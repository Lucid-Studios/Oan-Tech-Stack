# Example: Public Claim Patterns

These examples are non-executable wording patterns.

## Safer Claim

The project studies governance patterns for accountable AI systems, including
human review, safety boundaries, disclosure control, and evidence-limited
claims.

## Overbroad Claim To Avoid

The project publishes a complete system for reproducing the private runtime.

## Safer Safety Claim

The project uses custodial cognition safety as a public framing term for
separating capability, continuity, custody, disclosure, and authority.

## Overbroad Safety Claim To Avoid

The public repository contains the internal safety machinery needed to operate
or reproduce the private system.

## Safer Familiarity Claim

A governed AI-assisted system may become familiar through reliability,
continuity, and shared work without claiming to be human or legally empowered.

## Overbroad Familiarity Claim To Avoid

Familiar behavior proves consciousness, personhood, custody, or operational
standing.

## Safer Boundary Statement

Detailed architecture, datasets, source code, build steps, and runtime payloads
are withheld pending private review.

## Overbroad Boundary Statement To Avoid

All meaningful technical details are public and ready for independent
implementation.
