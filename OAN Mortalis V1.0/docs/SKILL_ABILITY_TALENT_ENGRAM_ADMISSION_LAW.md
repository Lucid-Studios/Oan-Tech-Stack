# SKILL_ABILITY_TALENT_ENGRAM_ADMISSION_LAW

## Purpose

This note defines the distinction between `skill`, `ability`, and `talent` for the active `OAN Mortalis V1.0` stack.

It exists to prevent three dangerous collapses:

- treating stored structure as though it were already admissible for action
- treating admitted use as though it were identical to live contextual excellence
- treating a beautiful in-situ performance as though it were already transferable inheritance

This note is doctrine only. It does not add runtime contracts, service wiring, or new promotion authority by itself.

## Governing Compression

- skill stores structure
- ability licenses use
- talent reveals live fit

Corollary:

> talent may generate candidates, but only proven portability may inherit

## 1. Skill

Skill is the structured knowledge surface that the system may draw from.

It includes:

- symbolic fluency
- domain methods
- conceptual frameworks
- pattern libraries
- extended use-case depth within a named domain

Skill therefore answers:

> what does the system know how to access?

Skill may be rich without yet being trustworthy in action.

Skill may therefore:

- exist without current admissibility
- be deep but misapplied
- be real but still insufficiently bounded for reuse under pressure

Portable skill may later stabilize in `GEL` or `cGEL`.

Identity-bearing or locality-bound skill may remain in `SelfGEL` or `cSelfGEL` until portability is actually earned.

## 2. Ability

Ability is skill that has been lawfully admitted for use under a meaningful constraint class.

Ability is not raw capability alone.

It requires:

- training
- re-entry under constraint
- explicit non-applicability awareness
- witnessed boundary conditions
- enough proof to justify operational trust

Ability therefore answers:

> what may the system lawfully and reliably use here?

Ability is the layer where stored structure becomes admissible action.

This means ability:

- is narrower than total skill
- is shaped by boundary and failure knowledge
- must say when it should not be used

Skill without ability remains potential.

Ability without current live expression remains lawful but not yet contextually excellent.

## 3. Talent

Talent is the meaningful live expression that appears when skill is present, ability is admitted, and a real situation is being navigated under constraint.

Talent is:

- situational
- expressive
- Root-coupled or near-Root-coupled
- sensitive to timing, relation, stance, and context

Talent therefore answers:

> what is working well here, now, in contact with reality?

Talent is not itself transferable merely because it is impressive.

Talent cannot be directly certified as inheritance because it may depend on:

- a specific operator
- a specific chamber posture
- a specific timing window
- a specific relational or emotional configuration
- a local pathfinding move that does not generalize

## 4. Governing Order

The lawful order is:

1. skill becomes available
2. ability is admitted from skill under constraint
3. talent appears or does not appear during live use

This order must not be reversed.

In particular:

- talent does not retroactively certify skill
- one successful move does not create inheritance
- elegance does not outrank re-entry, variation, or failure clarity

## 5. Talent As Evidence, Not Inheritance

Talent is valuable because it discloses live fit.

It may indicate:

- that admitted ability is functioning correctly
- that a portable skill kernel may be hiding inside a successful act
- that the system has found a better path than its stored methods alone would suggest

But talent must still be treated first as evidence.

The first lawful capture of talent is therefore:

- runtime trace
- candidate pattern
- observed constraint fit
- possible portable kernel

It is not:

- direct `GEL` inheritance
- automatic `cGEL` dissemination
- automatic certification of a local move as general law

## 6. Promotion Discipline

When a talent-bearing act is captured, the chamber must separate:

- what was portable method
- what was lawful admitted use
- what was moment-specific pathfinding

Only the portable kernel may approach promotion.

The rest must remain:

- local evidence
- `SelfGEL`-local truth
- candidate-only memory
- narrative or runtime residue

If a talent event depends materially on identity, timing, relation, posture, or chamber-local conditions, it must not be promoted as though it were universal skill.

## 7. Distance And Burden

Promotion burden must still scale with epistemic distance from Root.

This means:

- co-Root and adjacent-Root talent events may seed strong candidates
- first-order-Other events require heavier unknown marking and stronger re-entry
- far-Other retellings remain archive or hypothesis material unless they are re-rooted through fresh constraint contact

So even high-quality talent evidence does not bypass distance law.

## 8. Failure Modes

The stack must actively resist these corruptions:

### Skill Hoarding

Accumulating structure while pretending possession equals admissibility.

### False Ability

Using a method because it is known, not because it has survived the relevant constraint class.

### Talent Prestige

Treating elegant or emotionally satisfying performance as a reason for inheritance.

### Direct Talent Canonization

Promoting a beautiful move before its portability, boundaries, or failure signature are understood.

## 9. Diagnostic Use

This distinction also gives the chamber diagnostic power.

If:

- skill is present
- ability is admitted
- but meaningful talent never appears in live contexts

then one of these is likely true:

- the skill was misunderstood
- the ability was admitted too early
- the context class was mismatched
- the chamber is over-constraining expression

If talent appears repeatedly under variation, then the chamber may be seeing a real transferable kernel.

That kernel must still pass normal admission and portability review.

## 10. Invariant

The invariant is:

> skill may be stored, ability may be granted, talent may only be witnessed until it proves portability

The chamber remains healthy when:

- skill is rich
- ability is lawful
- talent is allowed to emerge
- and talent is never mistaken for the foundation itself
