# Jurisdiction Envelope And Transition Law

## Theorem

Kind remains stable; jurisdiction varies lawfully.

This layer does not replace bridge law. It sits beside bridge law as the deployment and review envelope for the same `SLI/EC` substrate.

## Constitutional Clarifications

1. Jurisdiction envelope law is parallel to, and may not weaken, bridge legality.
2. Surface projection is derived only from authoritative prior receipts through pure shared policy in `Oan.Common`.
3. Transition widening is never locally inferred by `AgentiCore`, worker lanes, ingress harnesses, or reducers.
4. `Allow`, `Hold`, and `Refuse` are first-class lawful outcomes, not partial failures.
5. Jurisdiction events are governance-retained and are not identity-forming memory.
6. Declared enum membership does not imply runtime reachability.
7. `WitnessOnly = true` permits classification, audit, and routing only. By itself it never authorizes promotion, publication widening, subordinate CME authorization, or retention expansion.

## Governance Lattice

Surface classes are governance envelopes, not branding labels. Each class is distinguished by:

- reveal ceiling
- oversight burden
- retention burden
- deployment audience

Initial class meanings:

- `Actualized`: canonical core identity under maximum continuity discipline
- `Industrialized`: repeated operational work under bounded identity anchoring
- `Civic`: public-facing reduced trust surface with conservative reveal law
- `Private`: enterprise/proprietary surface with bounded custody and disclosure partitioning
- `Government`: regulated sovereign-adjacent surface with maximal audit and oversight
- `Special`: high-risk domain surface reserved behind explicit dedicated policy

## Projection Law

Sprint 1 directly projects only:

- `Actualized` from first-boot governance layers with role-bound ECE formation
- `Industrialized` from protected-ingress runs with lawful bridge review, candidate-only runtime ceiling, and operator-formation evidence

Declared but not directly reachable in Sprint 1:

- `Civic`
- `Private`
- `Government`
- `Special`

## Transition Law

Transitions are evaluated through pure shared policy.

Supported decisions in this arc:

- `Actualized -> Industrialized`
- `Industrialized -> Civic`
- `Industrialized -> Private`
- `Industrialized -> Government`

Unsupported or premature transitions fail closed through `Hold` or `Refuse`.

## Invariant Spine

Every transition receipt must preserve:

- `identity-continuity-unchanged`
- `bond-realization-unchanged`
- `bridge-legality-remains-primary`
- `witness-only-does-not-authorize`
- `reveal-ceiling-may-not-widen-without-explicit-authority`
- `subordinate-cme-authorization-unchanged-unless-separately-authorized`
- `retention-burden-may-only-harden-on-stricter-surfaces`
- `no-local-runtime-inference-of-surface-promotion`

## Accountability Split

Jurisdiction denial or withdrawal events follow the same constitutional split as other protective actions:

- hot path: lightweight runtime telemetry
- cold path: governance-retained event lineage

The event is remembered for governance. Hostile payload is not retained as identity-forming memory.
