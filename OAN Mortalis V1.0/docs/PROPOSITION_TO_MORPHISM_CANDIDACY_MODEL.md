## 1. Morphism Candidacy

Morphism candidacy is the threshold at which a relation between stabilized localities may become a witness-bearing candidate for lawful correspondence.

It is not yet:

- transport itself
- admissibility
- governed identity
- Sanctuary accountability

## 2. Witness-Bearing Correspondence

Witness-bearing correspondence means the stack can explicitly name how one stabilized locality may be related to another without flattening them into one ambient sameness.

At doctrine level, such correspondence must be able to name:

- source locality
- target locality
- the invariants that must survive
- the lawful differences that may remain
- the residue that cannot be transported as though it survived

This is a doctrine description, not a runtime schema.

## 3. Preserved Invariants

A morphism candidate must preserve the invariants that make lawful comparison possible.

These may include:

- proposition-bearing anchors
- procedural ordering where relevant
- self-reference and other-reference polarity where required
- relation identity without identity collapse
- qualifier and context obligations
- boundary and reveal posture needed for later accountability

If the candidate cannot say what survives, it is not yet a lawful candidate.

## 4. Lawful Difference

Lawful difference is the part of cross-local relation that may change without destroying the correspondence.

Lawful difference may include:

- branch-local variation
- contextual reorientation
- rule-bounded transformation of form
- exploratory residue that has been properly marked rather than silently preserved

Lawful difference must not be used to excuse:

- invariant loss that was never disclosed
- counterfeit sameness
- hidden identity mutation

## 5. Residue Law

Residue is the material that does not survive as part of the lawful correspondence.

Residue must remain:

- explicit
- bounded
- non-counterfeit

Residue must not be treated as though it transported successfully.

This includes:

- branch-local artifacts
- exploratory scaffolding
- non-participatory remainder
- non-transportable local constraints

## 6. Gluing Thresholds

Gluing becomes thinkable only when the candidate relation is strong enough to hold lawful continuity without erasing local difference.

A gluing threshold therefore requires:

- explicit witness-bearing correspondence
- preserved invariants
- lawful handling of difference
- explicit residue boundaries
- no premature identity collapse

Gluing is stronger than casual comparison and weaker than automatic identity.

## 7. Boundary Between Candidacy And Lawful Transport

Morphism candidacy is a threshold of eligibility.

Lawful transport is a later threshold of actual crossing.

The boundary matters:

- candidacy says a lawful relation may be available
- transport says a lawful crossing has actually occurred
- transport still does not imply identity formation by default

Lawful transport yields an admissible surface, identity-bearing only where applicable.

That admissible surface remains distinct from:

- governed identity
- Sanctuary accountability
- custody mutation by default

## 8. Relationship To Sanctuary

Sanctuary remains later than morphism candidacy and later than lawful transport.

Sanctuary governs accountability over what survived prior thresholds strongly enough to become observable, admissible, and answerable.

Sanctuary does not:

- generate locality
- generate rehearsal
- generate witness-bearing correspondence
- treat transport as automatic identity formation
