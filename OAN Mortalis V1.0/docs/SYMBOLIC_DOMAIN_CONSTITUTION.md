# SYMBOLIC_DOMAIN_CONSTITUTION

## Purpose

This document defines the first symbolic governance layer above the canonical Atlas.

Its job is to reserve symbolic space before the first seed `GEL` begins carrying broad symbolic contextualization. The goal is to prevent the seed atlas from behaving as if every unused symbol is available for constructor law by default.

The governing rule is:

- only designated constructor/operator space is owned by default
- major disciplinary symbol domains are reserved and typed
- any bridge across symbolic domains must be explicit

## Top-Level Symbolic Domains

The first bounded symbolic constitution recognizes five domain classes:

1. `GrammarOperator`
   - globally reserved grammar and constructor operators
2. `RootNativeCore`
   - atlas-owned root cores and invariant predicate anchors
3. `DisciplinaryReserved`
   - major civilizational symbol domains that are reserved and opt-in bridged
4. `GovernanceMeta`
   - refusal, ambiguity, lineage, quarantine, and governance/control symbols
5. `ExperimentalExtension`
   - bounded research-local symbolic sandbox space

These classes are contract-backed in:

- `src/GEL.Contracts/Models/SymbolicGovernanceContracts.cs`

## First Reservation Rule

The symbolic space must not be governed by:

- all symbols are ours unless blocked

It must be governed by:

- grammar operator space is globally reserved
- root cores are atlas-owned
- disciplinary symbol domains are reserved by default
- governance/meta symbols are reserved for system law and control
- experimental/local extension stays sandboxed unless explicitly promoted

## Major Disciplinary Reserved Domains

The first build should treat these as globally reserved domain spaces:

- law
- medicine
- mathematics
- science
- physics

Additional candidates likely follow later:

- theology
- sociology
- logic
- linguistics
- computing

These are not default constructor glyph pools. They require bridge policy if they are to appear in canonical merged constructs.

## Constructor Body Law

The canonical symbolic object is the constructor body, not the merged render form.

That means:

- prefix operators are structural
- root core is structural
- suffix operators are structural
- disciplinary overlays, when later admitted, are structural
- merged glyphs are rendered artifacts only

The canonical Atlas therefore preserves triplets first and merged symbolic forms only as optional render surfaces.

## Collision and Bridge Policy

Collision law:

- no canonical merged construct may collide with reserved governance/meta symbols
- no canonical merged construct may collide with reserved disciplinary symbol domains unless explicitly bridged

Bridge law:

- bridges must be typed by source and target domain
- disciplinary reservation does not imply prohibition, but it does imply opt-in bridge policy
- experimental extension may not silently promote into canonical atlas-owned or disciplinary reserved space

## Merge Policy

Merged symbolic forms are allowed only when:

- constructor roles are lawful for the source domains involved
- no reserved-domain collision occurs
- merge policy for the participating domains permits rendering

Merged glyphs must never replace the structural constructor body as the meaning-bearing object.

## Immediate Consequence

The seed `GEL` may now be built under a stronger symbolic law:

- reserved grammar
- reserved disciplinary domains
- reserved governance/meta symbols
- root-native symbolic anchors
- explicit extension space

This is the minimum symbolic constitution needed before broader symbolic contextualization generation.
