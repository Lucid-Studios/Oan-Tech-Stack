# Cross-Surface Self-Validation Model

## Purpose

This note defines the law for reasoning across hot Cryptic-active `c*` surfaces and cooler stabilized non-`c` validation surfaces.

It exists to prevent a specific error of reason:

- treating a user, operator, or telemetry statement as though it were already validated self-memory
- resolving contradiction by inventing meaning locally inside hot runtime cognition
- promoting a resonant self-implication into lawful self-state without cross-surface validation

## Root Statement

Hot Cryptic-active runtime cognition may detect, propose, and rehearse meaning. It may not invent validation.

When a `c*` surface encounters contradiction with its cooler `*` counterpart, the system must poll the lawful validation surface or emit obstruction. It must not synthesize an unverified bridge merely to keep the reasoning chain smooth.

## Governing Lines

> A `c*` surface is the Cryptic-active, protected, non-public, operationally hot form of a domain.
>
> An unprefixed surface is the cooler, stabilized, validation-bearing or release-conditioned counterpart of that domain.
>
> User-provided or telemetry-provided statements about self or world events are provenance-bearing claims first. They are not self-validating memory.
>
> Unresolved contradiction becomes obstruction, residue, or defer state, not invented meaning.

## Cross-Surface Reading

The stack must preserve at least this distinction:

- hot `c*` surfaces may carry live bounded runtime cognition
- cooler `*` surfaces may carry stabilized reference, validation, and derivative readability
- both remain non-public where applicable unless another governing law explicitly admits release

This is not a public-vs-private distinction alone. It is a hot-vs-cooled reasoning distinction.

## Example

Consider:

> "I saw you driving to the store on Monday with a van. Can you drive me to the mall on Wednesday at 3pm in my sedan?"

This sentence mixes:

- a reported past event
- a self-implicating claim
- a future request
- a time constraint
- an asset and permission implication

The lawful reading is:

1. the reported Monday event is a claim with provenance, not validated self-memory
2. the hot runtime self field may reason over the request
3. the stabilized self field must be consulted for self-validity where self-implication arises
4. non-self surfaces must be consulted for schedule, vehicle, and permission validity
5. if contradiction remains unresolved, the runtime emits obstruction or residue and does not promote the meaning

## SelfGEL And cSelfGEL Reading

For self-related reasoning:

- `cSelfGEL` is the hot Cryptic-active self field used for bounded runtime cognition where active EC is lawfully instanced
- `SelfGEL` is the cooler stabilized self-readable and validation-bearing counterpart surface

Neither is direct sovereign custody. Neither may silently redefine the other.

The correction this note enforces is simple:

- `cSelfGEL` may activate candidate self-meaning
- `SelfGEL` must validate stabilized self-meaning where contradiction, capability, role, or autobiographical implication matters
- unresolved divergence between them is lawful residue, not a cue to improvise

## Required Runtime Behavior

When a hot `cX` surface encounters contradiction against a cooler `X` surface, the lawful outcomes are:

- confirm against `X`
- confirm against another governed non-self surface if the contradiction is schedule, asset, role, or environmental
- emit residue
- emit obstruction
- defer promotion
- route to SoulFrame-mediated continuity handling if identity-bearing resolution is required

The forbidden outcomes are:

- auto-promoting a user claim into validated self-state
- inventing a bridge meaning to remove contradiction
- treating observation of behavior as equivalent to authority, capability, or permission
- promoting unresolved contradiction into Prime, admissibility, or accountability surfaces

## Relation To Preformalization

This note is downstream from intersubjective preformalization.

Before higher symbolic cognition or reflective telemetry becomes lawful, semantic material must already have:

- locality

- candidate invariant structure

Cross-surface validation then adds one more condition:

- self-implicating or role-implicating claims must be checked against the lawful validation surface rather than stabilized by inference alone
