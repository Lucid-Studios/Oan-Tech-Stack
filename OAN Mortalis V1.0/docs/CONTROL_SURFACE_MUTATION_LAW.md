# Control Surface Mutation Law

## Purpose

This note defines how actionable content may reach the control plane without allowing connective transit or hot symbolic participation to inherit mutation authority merely by carrying that content.

It exists to prevent the central transit spine, the host membrane, or the hot symbolic runtime from gradually becoming a hidden sovereign through proximity to governing surfaces.

## Boundary

This note does not make:

- CradleTek the semantic mind
- C# the cognition engine
- transit envelopes into commit paths by themselves
- AgentiCore or SLI/Lisp into direct governors of control surfaces

This note does define:

- what counts as actionable content
- what kinds of control surfaces exist
- who may propose mutation
- who may authorize mutation
- what receipts are required
- what transit layers may never do

## Root Statement

The connective tissue may carry actionable content to the control plane, but it may not directly rewrite the control plane merely by virtue of carrying traffic through it.

## Governing Lines

> Transit is not authority.
>
> Carriage is not mutation.
>
> Passage is not admission.
>
> No connective layer may gain mutation authority merely by being on the path of transit.

## Why Control Surfaces Remain Typed

The active stack uses C# control surfaces so governance-facing mutation law can remain:

- typed
- explicit
- receipt-bearing
- mutation-resistant by default
- distinguishable from hot symbolic runtime behavior

In this role, C# is not the mind.

It is the control law and boundary grammar.

## Control Surface Classes

The minimum control-surface classes are:

- legality surfaces
- continuity and self-state surfaces
- lifecycle and service posture surfaces
- conditioning and release surfaces
- collapse and adjudication intake surfaces
- governance disposition surfaces

Each class may receive actionable content, but each class must still decide mutation through explicit governing operations.

## Actionable Content

Actionable content is any packet, request, proposal, event, or candidate that could lawfully lead to a control-surface change if admitted through the correct governing operation.

This may include:

- service requests
- runtime posture change requests
- protected dispatch requests
- validation requests
- return packets
- collapse packets
- refusal events
- adjudication candidates
- conditioning candidates

Actionable content is not self-enacting merely because it is well-formed or transit-visible.

## Proposal Rights

The following layers may propose actionable content:

- Sanctuary-facing service surfaces
- CradleTek transit and lifecycle surfaces
- SoulFrame-mediated legality and continuity paths
- AgentiCore bounded cognition
- SLI/Lisp hot symbolic runtime

Proposal rights do not equal mutation rights.

## Authorization Rights

Authorization rights remain with the lawful governing surface for the relevant class.

Examples:

- SoulFrame may authorize or refuse legality-sensitive self-state and continuity changes
- CradleTek may authorize or refuse service posture, lifecycle, and transit handling changes that belong to runtime governance
- Steward-guided or later governance surfaces may authorize or refuse adjudicative outcomes
- conditioning and release surfaces may authorize or refuse Prime-facing derivative release

No layer gains those rights simply because it carried the packet.

## Mutation Sequence

The lawful sequence is:

1. actionable content is formed
2. it is placed in a governed envelope
3. the envelope transits through CradleTek where governance-relevant passage applies
4. the appropriate control surface receives it as a request, candidate, or event
5. the governing operation evaluates it
6. an explicit decision authorizes, refuses, defers, or quarantines mutation
7. only then may the lawful surface change state
8. the mutation emits a receipt or refusal back through the appropriate return path

This sequence prevents carriage from being misread as lawful mutation.

## Transit-Layer Prohibitions

Transit or connective layers may not:

- silently rewrite control surfaces
- self-promote transit events into lawful mutation
- treat packet arrival as equivalent to authorized state change
- mutate stable governing surfaces because a packet looked persuasive
- use centrality of routing as justification for constitutional authorship

## Hot Runtime Prohibitions

Hot symbolic runtime layers may not:

- declare admission by self-coherence alone
- mutate control-plane truth unilaterally
- treat generated proposals as authorized just because they are internally consistent
- bypass lawful return, conditioning, or adjudication surfaces

## Receipt Law

Meaningful control-surface mutation must remain receipt-bearing.

Minimum outcome classes are:

- authorized and applied
- refused
- deferred
- quarantined
- noop

Receipts must preserve:

- provenance
- relevant posture or policy context
- governing surface identity
- decision class
- any residue or follow-up requirement
