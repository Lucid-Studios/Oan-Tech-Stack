# STACK_AUTHORITY_AND_MUTATION_LAW

## Purpose

This document defines the lawful authority and mutation boundaries of the active `OAN Mortalis V1.0` stack.

It turns the system ontology into direct rules:

- who may host
- who may mediate
- who may propose
- who may commit
- who may only observe
- which direct mutation paths are forbidden

Assessment date:

- March 6, 2026

## Root Law

No layer may silently seize the authority of another layer.

All consequential change must move through an explicit lawful path.

When authority is unclear, the stack must refuse, freeze, quarantine, noop, or escalate rather than silently reconcile by mutation.

This also applies to Theater locality: work may proceed freely inside a lawful Theater, but movement between Theaters requires explicit lawful transformation.

This also applies to Prime and Cryptic authority: Cryptic may lawfully yield Prime derivatives, but Prime may not unveil, reconstruct, or govern Cryptic as sovereign substance.

## Layer Law

### CradleTek Law

CradleTek may:

- host, isolate, mount, constrain, and recover runtime services
- manage local seed LLM selection, download, integration, and use
- host bounded flasked services under operator call and mission law
- maintain protected cryptic services such as `Mantle of Sovereigns`
- form, encode, and route governed call traffic for governance-relevant ingress, dispatch, return, and collapse transitions

CradleTek may not:

- define identity
- silently author GEL
- bypass SoulFrame and SLI governance for commitment-bearing state changes
- allow governance-relevant cross-boundary service traffic to bypass CradleTek transit handling

### SoulFrame Law

SoulFrame may:

- evaluate legality
- gate mount and write permissions
- enforce session, policy, and promotion boundaries
- freeze, quarantine, or halt the stack

SoulFrame may not:

- directly author identity-bearing state
- rewrite AgentiCore self-state by silent intervention
- act as autonomous cognition or runtime command

SoulFrame must remain:

- authoritative for legality
- non-authoritative for runtime cognition content

### AgentiCore Law

AgentiCore may:

- construct intents
- bind agent runtime behavior to a CME
- build or populate `SelfGEL` and `cSelfGEL` from governed sources
- produce proposals, packets, and action attempts

AgentiCore may not:

- directly commit GEL
- bypass SoulFrame or SLI
- silently redefine its own identity kernel
- autonomously self-thaw or self-authorize around runtime law

### SLI Law

SLI may:

- packetize, route, transform, and tensorize symbolic material
- emit evidence, refusals, and commit intents
- connect stack components to protected stores and modules through lawful symbolic interfaces
- qualify uptake candidates under theater-scoped conformance before any witness, transport, admissibility, or accountability claim becomes lawful
- form higher-order cognitive locality under bounded runtime law before any transport or identity claim becomes admissible

SLI may not:

- define identity
- seize runtime authority
- directly rewrite GEL
- directly rewrite AgentiCore or SoulFrame state outside lawful interfaces
- let tensor resonance bypass lawful conformance into actualized accountable objects
- promote user-provided or telemetry-provided self-claims into validated self-state without lawful cross-surface checking
- invent reconciliatory meaning when a hot `c*` surface contradicts its cooler validation counterpart
- let the host membrane silently inherit semantic authority for legal SLI programs that belong to the target runtime
- treat lawful transport as automatic identity formation

When semantic ownership transfer is in effect for a migrated SLI lane, the membrane may judge legality, but it may not judge meaning.

### Cross-Surface Self-Validation Law

When hot `c*` runtime cognition encounters contradiction against a cooler `*` validation surface, the stack may:

- poll the lawful validation surface
- poll another governed non-self surface when the contradiction concerns schedule, asset, capability, or permission
- emit residue or obstruction
- defer promotion
- route identity-bearing conflict to SoulFrame-mediated continuity handling

The stack may not:

- treat a user claim as validated self-memory merely because it is linguistically coherent
- treat observed behavior as equivalent to permission, capability, or authority
- invent local meaning to dissolve contradiction before validation
- promote unresolved contradiction into Prime, admissibility, or accountability surfaces

### Membrane And Target Runtime Law

For migrated SLI semantic lanes:

- the target runtime is meaning-authority
- the membrane is legality, transport, telemetry, conditioning, and return authority
- target absence must resolve to refusal or explicit unavailable status, not host semantic fallback

The membrane may therefore:

- reject
- quarantine
- trace
- receipt
- mediate host-device transport

The membrane may not:

- reinterpret a legal program's meaning
- invent cognition results when target semantics are unavailable
- quietly execute host semantics under target naming

## Governed Call Transit Law

All governance-relevant ingress, dispatch, return, and collapse traffic must pass through CradleTek for lawful call formation, telemetry encoding, lifecycle control, and return normalization.

This law does not require every internal helper call to route through CradleTek.

It does require CradleTek passage for:

- public-facing service ingress into protected or governance-relevant lanes
- protected dispatch into AgentiCore or target SLI/Lisp runtime work
- return traffic carrying traces, residues, receipts, or conditioned derivatives
- collapse traffic carrying protected closure or adjudication candidates

No component may bypass this transit spine for convenience once a governance-relevant boundary is being crossed.

Governed transit does not grant mutation authority over the control surfaces touched by that traffic.

## Control Surface Mutation Law

Control surfaces exist so the connective tissue can transport, witness, request, and receipt actionable content without silently redefining the governing surfaces by participating in them.

This means:

- actionable content may be proposed from hot symbolic or connective layers
- transit may carry that content through governed envelopes
- only the lawful control surface may authorize, refuse, defer, or quarantine mutation
- no transit layer gains mutation rights merely by proximity to the control surface

Short rule:

> Transit is not authority. Carriage is not mutation. Passage is not admission.

## Documentation Governance Law

The active build repo records executable truth and immediate implementation constraints.

The `Documentation Repo` records stabilized conceptual truth and theory digestion for the governed build cycle.

Governed build work must consult both when the `Documentation Repo` is available.

Documentation uptake may guide future implementation, but it may not silently override executable runtime truth.

When the two surfaces diverge, the stack must record doctrine debt, build debt, or migration debt rather than inventing reconciliation by narrative convenience.

### GEL Law

GEL:

- is shared invariant state
- is append-only
- is identity-bearing
- must not contain ephemeral runtime cognition

Only governed commit paths may change GEL.

### Cryptic Law

The cryptic side of the stack is the explicit private operational side managed through SLI.

Cryptic domains include:

- raw data
- pretrained engram payloads
- chat logs
- encrypted but not yet data-rights-processed constructs
- engineered cognition telemetry
- `c*` surfaces such as `cGEL`, `cGoA`, and `cSelfGEL`

Cryptic material may:

- be stored
- be processed
- be routed
- be audited
- be transformed into governed proposals

Cryptic material may not:

- silently become Prime-safe
- silently become GEL-admitted
- bypass rights processing, governance checks, or lawful promotion paths

### OE Law

OE:

- is local, relative, provisional, and non-authoritative by default
- may hold working structures, notes, overlays, and mission-local expansion
- must not silently rewrite GEL
- must not bind identity or public commitment by default

### Prime Law

Prime is the public-facing or shared-use side of the stack.

Prime-safe material may include:

- redacted pointers
- encrypted release-ready material
- admitted engram forms in GEL
- standard-plane or public operational context

Prime may not be treated as:

- a direct dump target for Cryptic state
- an automatic mirror of private cognition telemetry
- an excuse to bypass explicit admission and release law

## Allowed Mutation Paths

### Path 1. Runtime Hosting

`CradleTek -> runtime surfaces`

Meaning:

- lawful hosting, scheduling, resource shaping, storage mounting, and service containment

### Path 2. Mission And Session Gating

`SoulFrame -> legality decisions -> allowed stack behavior`

Meaning:

- SoulFrame may authorize, refuse, freeze, quarantine, or halt

### Path 3. Operational Proposal

`AgentiCore -> intent/proposal -> SLI packetization -> SoulFrame evaluation`

Meaning:

- AgentiCore may act only through lawful proposal and packet paths

### Path 4. Symbolic Transport

`SLI -> gate evidence and commit intent -> governed commit path`

Meaning:

- SLI may carry the structure of a proposed change
- it does not own the identity change itself

### Path 5. Commit Crystallization

`authorized commit -> GEL append`

Meaning:

- identity-bearing and shared invariant changes occur only here

### Path 6. Runtime Reconstruction

`GEL -> admitted reconstruction -> GoA or runtime state`

Meaning:

- operational state may be recovered from governed identity-bearing state

## Forbidden Direct Mutation Paths

The following direct paths are constitutionally forbidden unless an explicit temporary migration exception is recorded:

- `CradleTek -> GEL` direct ungated mutation
- `CradleTek -> AgentiCore identity` direct rewrite
- `CradleTek -> SoulFrame authority` seizure or bypass
- `SoulFrame -> AgentiCore identity kernel` direct silent rewrite
- `SoulFrame -> GEL` ungated authorship
- `AgentiCore -> GEL` direct commit
- `AgentiCore -> runtime thaw or privilege escalation` autonomous actuation
- `SLI -> GEL` direct identity mutation outside governed commit
- `SLI -> SoulFrame authority` override
- `SLI -> AgentiCore identity` direct rewrite
- `OE -> GEL` silent propagation
- telemetry -> autonomous runtime or identity actuation
- exploratory theater -> commitment-bearing identity bind without explicit transition

## Telemetry Law

Telemetry is advisory unless a governing contract explicitly states otherwise.

Telemetry may:

- inform operators
- support diagnostics
- provide forensic trails
- support refusal, freeze, quarantine, and review

Telemetry may not:

- autonomously trigger identity mutation
- autonomously change runtime governance posture
- silently alter GEL, GoA, or OE contents
- silently promote Cryptic matter into Prime

## CradleTek Local Agent Law

The active program may run three primary CradleTek local governing roles:

- `Mother`
- `Father`
- `Steward`

### Mother

Mother governs Prime-layer operational handling.

Mother may:

- manage public-facing and shared-use operational posture
- work with material that has already been redacted, pointerized, encrypted for release, or admitted into GEL-facing forms

Mother may not:

- unilaterally seize Cryptic custody
- silently expose private operational matter

### Father

Father governs the explicit Cryptic SLI side of the stack.

Father may:

- operate on raw data, pretrained payloads, chat logs, cryptic telemetry, and protected `c*` domains
- prepare lawful reach paths toward Prime through governed SLI and admission flows

Father may not:

- silently promote Cryptic matter into Prime
- bypass rights processing, SLI governance, or SoulFrame legality

### Steward

Steward governs CME monitoring and swarm facilitation.

Steward may:

- monitor CME agents
- facilitate additional swarm agent management
- support bounded labor structures, later including jobs, careers, trades, talents, skills, and abilities

Steward may not:

- bypass CME boundaries
- silently escalate swarm privilege
- convert facilitation into unauthorized authority seizure

## Triadic Witness Law

Mother, Father, and Steward are not one blended controller split across three labels.

They are jurisdictionally distinct witness roles:

- Mother witnesses Prime coherence and outward lawful presentation
- Father witnesses cryptic integrity, seals, custody, and internal safety
- Steward witnesses runtime continuity, orchestration, and operational structure

The stack should prefer triangulated governance over unilateral declaration.

## Role Override Law

The triadic roles may constrain within their lawful domains, but they must not casually override each other.

Examples:

- Mother may not weaken Father-bound security controls for public convenience
- Father may not censor lawful Prime behavior merely by owning cryptic custody
- Steward may not redefine discourse law or seal law merely by controlling execution continuity

Any cross-role override path must be:

- explicit
- audited
- governed by stack law
- bounded by refusal and halt semantics when legitimacy is unclear

## State Signal Law

Each triadic role should emit a governance state signal into the state resolution layer.

Minimum canonical state set:

- `GREEN`
- `YELLOW`
- `RED`
- `LOCK`

No single role should be able to declare the entire stack healthy in the presence of a higher-severity witness state from another role.

Illustrative precedence:

- any `LOCK` should halt or hard-stop the lawful flow
- `RED` in Father should be treated as a security-level integrity breach
- `RED` in Steward should be treated as structural runtime degradation
- `RED` in Mother should be treated as outward integrity or presentation failure

This is a governance-state law, not yet a claim that all production code already resolves these states automatically.

## Founding Witness Law

A lawfully formed Sanctuary runtime should begin with explicit witness formation events.

Minimum founding pattern:

1. Steward attests runtime continuity
2. Father attests cryptic boundary and seal posture
3. Mother attests Prime charter or outward posture

These founding acts should be treated as:

- witness attestations
- lawful formation markers
- OE chain genesis or startup record material when the stack records them

## Theater Law

### Prime Theater

Prime Theater is:

- the public operational universe
- commitment-eligible under explicit lawful transition
- governance-sensitive

Prime Theater outputs may become binding only through explicit lawful paths.

### Cryptic Theater

Cryptic Theater is:

- the protected interior universe
- security- and continuity-sensitive
- not automatically public or release-safe

Cryptic Theater outputs may influence Prime only through lawful Theater transition mechanisms and governed admission.

### Dream Theater

Dream Theater is:

- the generative exploratory universe
- suitable for speculation, hypothesis generation, branching, and creative exploration
- non-binding by default

Dream Theater outputs may not:

- silently become Prime facts
- silently become Cryptic guarantees
- silently bind identity continuity
- silently become admissible surfaces or Sanctuary accountability claims

## Higher-Order Locality Preconditions

Before cross-Theater transport becomes lawful, the stack must preserve higher-order locality formation as an explicit sequence:

1. proposition
2. procedure
3. perspectival configuration
4. participatory configuration
5. bounded Dream/Game rehearsal
6. morphism candidacy
7. lawful transport
8. admissible surface, identity-bearing only where applicable
9. Sanctuary-governed accountability

This means:

- propositional and procedural layers are stabilized floor layers
- perspectival and participatory configuration are preconditions of morphism candidacy
- Dream/Game rehearsal is exploratory inhabitation, not truth, identity binding, admissibility, or Sanctuary intake
- Sanctuary remains the accountability boundary over what survived prior thresholds

Governing warning:

> The system must not attempt transport across localities it has not yet lawfully inhabited.

## Theater-Scoped Uptake Law

Before witness or transport becomes lawful, SLI may perform theater-scoped uptake inside a lawful Theater.

That uptake remains a bounded conformance seam between tensor resonance and SLI/Lisp actualization.

It may:

- type a candidate by lawful engram kind
- declare observer-position and relative carrier posture
- derive local atlas and functor plans
- extract an invariant-bearing core
- refuse actualization through consistency or cohomology obstruction
- emit bounded uptake candidates or packets for later lanes

It may not:

- treat resonance as already witnessed
- treat uptake as already transported
- treat uptake as already admissible
- treat uptake as already accountable
- bypass identity, custody, `GEL`, or Sanctuary law

## Theater Transition Law

Each Theater is a local universe with its own geometry, ruleset, symbolic grammar, admissible actors, and lawful operations.

In stack terms, a Theater is an OAN locality surface whose conformance law is being deliberately derived from IUTT-level distinctions for engineered cognition.

Repo terms remain primary.

No literal one-to-one formal identity with Mochizuki's formal objects is claimed unless exact correspondences are explicitly specified.

Each Theater therefore acts as a distinct coordinate system for cognition.

Cross-Theater movement requires lawful transformation structures.

Those transformations must not be invoked as though locality formation were optional preamble.

Canonical transition structures:

- braiding
- comingling
- fiber bundles
- gluing

These structures exist to preserve required invariants during transition.

Operational interpretation:

- later Engineered Cognition surfaces may describe some of these transitions as role transitions or context switching
- that operational language does not eliminate the underlying constitutional requirement for lawful cross-Theater transformation
- those operational labels describe implementation posture, not permission to bypass locality law

Key principle:

- work may proceed freely inside a lawful Theater
- movement between Theaters requires lawful transformation
- lawful transport follows candidacy and yields an admissible surface, identity-bearing only where applicable

## Theater Pollution Law

The stack must prevent cross-Theater pollution.

Examples of forbidden pollution:

- Dream speculation silently entering Prime as fact
- Cryptic protected matter leaking into Prime discourse
- Cryptic verification structures being treated as public rhetorical content by default
- Prime presentation logic weakening cryptic or Dream protections for convenience

## Triadic Theater Alignment

The triadic witness roles align naturally to Theater integrity:

- Mother witnesses Prime Theater integrity
- Father witnesses Cryptic Theater integrity
- Steward witnesses lawful continuity across Theaters

Steward is therefore not merely a runtime monitor.

Steward is also responsible for continuity of lawful transition across the Theater bundle.

## Theater Artifact Law

Artifacts may carry more than one Theater projection when the governing gluing relation is explicit and lawful.

Example projection families include:

- visible projection for Prime
- structural metadata for Cryptic
- speculative overlays for Dream

Layered or holographic artifacts such as `.hopng` surfaces must preserve Theater-local distinctions rather than collapsing their projections into one undifferentiated payload.

When `.hopng` is used as a holographic data tool surface, it must be governed as a cross-Theater carrier rather than treated as a flat media file.

### Exploratory Theater

Exploratory modes such as OAN Theater or GoA-like rehearsal domains are:

- non-binding by default
- suitable for rehearsal, expansion, and provisional structure
- prohibited from silently binding identity continuity

## Flasking Law

Flasking is lawful only when:

- bounded by a CME
- operator-called or explicitly authorized
- constrained by CradleTek runtime law
- gated by SoulFrame legality
- pairbonded or mission-bound through AgentiCore where required

Flasking must not:

- create hidden identity authority
- leak local exploratory state into shared invariant state by default
- bypass the mission envelope

## Recovery Law

Recovery from frozen, quarantined, or halted states requires explicit authority.

There is no silent self-thaw path for:

- AgentiCore
- SLI
- family-local runtime services acting without governing authority

Recovery must be:

- explicit
- receipted
- auditable
- policy-bound

## Failure Law

When a boundary is violated, the stack should prefer:

1. refusal
2. noop
3. quarantine
4. freeze
5. halt

over:

- silent repair
- silent mutation
- hidden state reconciliation

## Acceptance Questions

Every new subsystem or major feature should be answerable against this law:

1. Is it runtime-authoritative, legality-authoritative, proposal-authoritative, or non-authoritative?
2. Is it identity-bearing, identity-coupled, identity-neutral, or identity-external?
3. What may it write directly?
4. What may it only observe?
5. What path must it use to influence GEL or GoA?
6. What does it do when authority is missing or disputed?
