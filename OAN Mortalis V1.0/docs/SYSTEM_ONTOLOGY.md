# SYSTEM_ONTOLOGY

## Purpose

This document defines the system ontology for the active `OAN Mortalis V1.0` stack.

It is the root description of what the major stack objects are allowed to be, not merely what they currently happen to do in code.

This ontology is intended to stabilize:

- layer identity
- authority identity
- mutation boundaries
- stack vocabulary
- executable identity versus system identity

Assessment date:

- March 6, 2026

## Identity Levels

### System Identity

- `OAN Tech Stack`

Meaning:

- the governed cognition and runtime stack as an architectural whole

### Product And Executable Identity

- `Sanctuary`
- intended executable identity: `Sanctuary.exe`

Meaning:

- one compiled application or product instantiation of the OAN stack

Constraint:

- executable identity must not be treated as the root ontological truth of the stack
- the stack ontology remains primary even when the executable product name is stable

## Root Constitutional Sentence

The OAN Tech Stack is a governed cognition stack in which runtime law, contextual mediation, operational cognition, and invariant meaning are structurally separated and may interact only through explicit lawful interfaces.

The same stack is also a governed bundle of local operational universes, called Theaters, whose cross-boundary movement must preserve lawful invariants across locality changes.

Within that bundle, Cryptic is the source domain of identity-bearing inscription and Prime is the processed derivative domain of lawful expression.

Within that same stack, Sanctuary provides a collective symbolic nervous system through SLI, while `OE` and `cOE` preserve the individuated continuity and custody of each CME.

The stack therefore distinguishes a prior coherence regime from later accountable surfaces:

- CradleTek hosts conditions
- `OE/cOE` act as continuity-binding conditions rather than flat modules
- `SelfGEL/cSelfGEL` appear as the first stabilized self-readable surfaces within that coherence regime
- proposition-bearing stabilization occurs before morphism candidacy
- lawful transport yields an admissible surface, identity-bearing only where applicable
- Sanctuary governs only what has localized enough to become proposition-bearing, observable, admissible, and accountable

The governing seam is formalized in `docs/WEAVE_TO_PROPOSITION_STABILIZATION_MODEL.md` as:

1. pre-instantiated coherence
2. proposition-bearing stabilization
3. admissible proposition surface
4. Sanctuary-governed accountability

The higher-order locality continuation of that seam is formalized through:

- `docs/PROPOSITION_TO_PERSPECTIVAL_CONFIGURATION_MODEL.md`
- `docs/PERSPECTIVAL_TO_PARTICIPATORY_ENGRAM_FORMATION_MODEL.md`
- `docs/BOUNDED_DREAM_GAME_REHEARSAL_MODEL.md`
- `docs/PROPOSITION_TO_MORPHISM_CANDIDACY_MODEL.md`

That continuation preserves this order:

1. proposition
2. procedure
3. perspectival configuration
4. participatory configuration
5. bounded Dream/Game rehearsal
6. morphism candidacy
7. lawful transport
8. admissible surface, identity-bearing only where applicable
9. Sanctuary-governed accountability

`CradleTek hosts. SoulFrame governs. AgentiCore inhabits. SLI/Lisp forms higher-order cognitive locality before it ever transports it.`

## Primary Stack Objects

### CradleTek

Ontological class:

- primary application, orchestration, and execution fabric

Primary purpose:

- compose and coordinate stack execution
- orchestrate bonded CME services and swarm behavior
- route runtime and service lifecycle behavior
- maintain infrastructure services and protected stores
- manage local seed LLM selection, retrieval, integration, and use
- host bonded CME instances and operator-called flasking services

Authority status:

- authoritative for runtime hosting, isolation, resource posture, service composition, and orchestration-bound execution

Identity relation:

- identity-external

Mutation rights:

- may host, constrain, start, stop, and isolate runtime processes and bounded services
- may manage infrastructure-backed stores and runtime mounts under governance
- may maintain cryptic shadow services such as `Mantle of Sovereigns`

Read rights:

- may read runtime configuration, store topology, admission metadata, and execution constraints needed to host lawful stack behavior

Write path:

- infrastructure writes must pass through lawful store and governance boundaries
- stack-level commits remain governed by SoulFrame and SLI gate paths where required

Prohibited actions:

- must not define identity
- must not silently redefine GEL or OE structures
- must not bypass stack governance to commit shared invariant state
- must not collapse self-state mediation, sovereign custody, and active cognition into one layer

Failure semantics:

- constrain, refuse, isolate, freeze, or halt according to stack governance mode

### SoulFrame

Ontological class:

Primary purpose:

- bind operator login and credential posture to lawful session behavior
- project mitigated self-state from sovereign custody into operational use
- receive and shape collapse or return intake for governed re-Engrammitization
- expose mission context, OE selection, and review posture
- regulate the authority boundary between runtime activity and governed commitment

Authority status:

- conditionally authoritative
- final enforcement authority for legality, admissibility, and self-state membrane checks at the governance boundary

Identity relation:

- identity-coupled but not identity-bearing

Mutation rights:

- may authorize or refuse lawful transitions
- may gate commits, promotions, mounts, and state transitions
- may freeze, quarantine, or halt when invariants are violated
- may project mitigated working-state without disclosing sovereign source substance

Read rights:

- may read mission posture, policy bundles, session mounts, and packet evidence needed to evaluate legality

Write path:

- may emit decisions, receipts, and enforcement state
- must remain ignorant of Layer-2 cognition internals beyond what is necessary for lawful verification

Prohibited actions:

- must not directly author identity-bearing state
- must not silently rewrite AgentiCore identity or OE contents
- must not act as an autonomous cognition engine
- must not collapse into a generic everything-service host

Failure semantics:

- refuse, freeze, quarantine, or halt rather than silently repairing or mutating around a breach

### AgentiCore

Primary purpose:

Authority status:

- conditionally authoritative for operational cognition inside the mission envelope

Identity relation:

- identity-coupled and operationally identity-bearing inside the workspace, but not the sovereign root

Mutation rights:

- may construct intents, proposals, and bounded runtime state
- may assemble self-state and mission-local cognition structures under governance

Read rights:

- may read admitted mission context, operator-bonded CME metadata, permitted store outputs, and lawful symbolic material

Write path:

- emits governed intents and action attempts
- must not bypass SLI and SoulFrame to commit identity or shared invariant structures

Prohibited actions:

- must not silently redefine its own kernel identity
- must not directly rewrite GEL
- must not self-thaw or self-promote around runtime governance
- must not directly touch sovereign source custody as if it were the canonical author

Failure semantics:

- refuse, noop, escalate, or request review

- identity-neutral

- may produce symbolic traces, decision structures, and candidate engram structures inside bounded runtime cognition
- may not directly commit identity, governance, or custody change by itself

Read rights:

- may read admitted symbolic material, policy bundles, handle registries, and resolution metadata

Write path:

- may emit symbolic packets, evidence, refusals, routing outputs, symbolic traces, and candidate engram-bearing structures

### Prime And Cryptic Split

The stack is explicitly split across two major operational sides:

- Prime
- Cryptic

Prime side:

- public-facing or shared-use space
- redacted, pointerized, encrypted-for-release, or admitted engram outputs
- GEL and standard GoA-facing consequences
- processed and checked data that is lawful for Prime handling under data practice

Cryptic side:

- raw data
- pretrained engram payloads
- chat logs
- encrypted but not yet data-rights-processed constructs
- engineered cognition telemetry
- cryptic-native SLI handling
- live unmasked data still subject to protected data practices

Governing rule:

- the Cryptic side is not a staging synonym for Prime
- movement from Cryptic to Prime requires lawful SLI mediation and governed admission

### Prime And Cryptic Data Topology

Prime-side CradleTek service surfaces include:

- `GoA`
- `GEL`
- `MoS`

These are the hosted service-plane surfaces for:

- primary Engrammitization
- internal agentic use
- external CME construction
- redacted or encrypted pointer publication
- Prime-safe telemetry and metacognitive access

Cryptic-side CradleTek sovereign surfaces include:

- `cGoA`

These are the protected custody and processing surfaces for:

- pre-Engrammitized processing

Topology rule:

- Prime service planes are not the totality of identity-bearing reality
- Cryptic sovereign planes are not merely darker caches of Prime
- SoulFrame governs the membrane between sovereign custody and operational use
- AgentiCore operates on mitigated working self-state, not on raw sovereign custody directly

## Triadic Governance Principle

The three CradleTek local governing roles form a triadic governance architecture.

The purpose of the triad is:

- no single role should define public coherence, cryptic integrity, and runtime continuity at the same time
- each role witnesses a distinct axis of stack health
- global stack state should be resolved from triangulated witness signals rather than asserted by one subsystem alone

The roles map to three integrity questions:

| Role | Domain | Governing Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `Mother` | Prime and outward presentation | Is the system acting coherently and responsibly outwardly? |
| `Father` | Cryptic and boundary integrity | Is the system internally safe and uncompromised? |
| `Steward` | Sanctuary runtime continuity | Is the system structurally operating correctly? |

## Triangulated State Engine

Each role is expected to emit a runtime governance state signal.

Minimum canonical state set:

- `GREEN`
- `YELLOW`
- `RED`
- `LOCK`

Interpretation:

- `GREEN` means operationally acceptable
- `YELLOW` means caution or constrained concern
- `RED` means active integrity concern in that role's witness domain
- `LOCK` means immediate non-negotiable stop condition

Global state should be resolved from the combined witness pattern rather than from one local claim of health.

Illustrative resolution model:

| Mother | Father | Steward | Global Result |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `GREEN` | `GREEN` | `GREEN` | Operational |
| `YELLOW` | `GREEN` | `GREEN` | Caution |
| `GREEN` | `RED` | `GREEN` | Security Alert |
| `GREEN` | `GREEN` | `RED` | Runtime Degraded |
| `RED` | `RED` | any | Critical |
| `LOCK` | any | any | Halt |

This resolution model is constitutional guidance for future implementation, not yet a claim that all runtime code already enforces it.

## Role Independence

The triadic roles are not arbitrary aliases for the same authority.

They must remain jurisdictionally distinct:

- Mother must not weaken cryptic or security law merely for public convenience
- Father must not suppress lawful public or Prime behavior merely by owning the interior boundary
- Steward must not redefine discourse law, seal law, or identity law merely by controlling runtime continuity

Each role may:

- observe
- report
- constrain within its domain
- emit witness signals

Each role must not:

- unilaterally seize the authority domain of the others

## Glyphic Acts And Founding Witness Events

When a Sanctuary instance forms lawfully, the founding runtime should emit witness events for the triad.

Canonical founding order:

1. Steward initializes the runtime continuity frame
2. Father verifies cryptic boundary and seal posture
3. Mother affirms Prime or outward charter posture

These acts may be treated as:

- glyphic acts
- founding witness events
- OE chain genesis markers for a lawful session

They are not cosmetic. They mark that the runtime was constituted under explicit governance rather than ambient state.

## Theaters And Mode Domains

### Theater

Ontological class:

- locally coherent universe of operation

Meaning:

- a Theater is not merely a UI mode or emotional posture
- it is a local universe with its own geometry, ruleset, symbolic grammar, admissible actors, and lawful operations
- it is an Inter-universal Techmuller Theory-aligned locality concept for universal selection inside the stack

Each Theater defines its own native:

- coordinates
- transformations
- reasoning structures
- artifact forms
- local reality and lawful selection conditions

Consequence:

- an operation valid in one Theater is not automatically valid in another
- movement between Theaters requires a lawful mapping, not a casual context flip
- each Theater therefore acts as a distinct coordinate system for cognition

### Prime Theater

Ontological class:

- public operational universe

Meaning:

- the Theater of discourse, interaction, artifact publication, and dialectic exchange
- the public or shared-use operational universe

Authority relation:

- Prime integrity is witnessed primarily by `Mother`

Constraint:

- Prime does not automatically inherit Dream speculation or Cryptic protected matter

### Cryptic Theater

Ontological class:

- protected interior universe

Meaning:

- the Theater of identity continuity, security boundaries, sealed memory, certification records, governance verification, and protected telemetry

Authority relation:

- Cryptic integrity is witnessed primarily by `Father`

Constraint:

- Cryptic operations may not silently surface into Prime

### Dream Theater

Ontological class:

- generative exploratory universe

Meaning:

- the Theater of speculation, hypothesis generation, creative exploration, and plural branching possibilities

Authority relation:

- Dream activity remains governed by stack law but is not Prime-safe by default

Constraint:

- Dream outputs are not automatically fit for Prime or Cryptic commitment
- they require lawful transformation before crossing Theater boundaries

### Theater Geometry

Each Theater has its own geometry.

This means:

- coordinates are local
- transformations are local
- reasoning structures are local
- artifacts may have Theater-specific lawful forms

Because geometry is local, Theater separation prevents:

- speculation contaminating facts
- protected interior matter leaking into discourse
- governance-only structures collapsing creativity or exploration

### Lawful Theater Transitions

Movement between Theaters must happen through lawful transition structures.

Canonical transition mechanisms include:

- braiding
- comingling
- fiber bundles
- gluing

Interpretation:

- these are structured compatibility relations that preserve required invariants across local universes
- later Engineered Cognition surfaces may describe these in operational terms such as role transition or context switching
- those later operational descriptions are secondary names for lawful locality transition, not permission to collapse Theater boundaries

Category-theoretic reading:

- a lawful Theater transition behaves like a constrained functor
- it maps an object from one Theater into a lawful form in another while preserving the invariants that must survive translation

Example:

- `Dream -> Prime`

Meaning:

- a speculative object becomes a discourse-fit object only if coherence, security, identity, and governance constraints survive the mapping

### Theater Multiplicity In Artifacts

A single artifact may contain multiple Theater projections at once.

Example:

- visible projection in Prime
- structural metadata in Cryptic
- speculative overlays in Dream

This means one object can exist in several local universes at once if the gluing relations are lawful.

This includes holographic or layered artifacts such as `.hopng` surfaces when the artifact explicitly defines the lawful gluing relation between Theater-local projections.

For current stack planning, `.hopng` should be treated as an emerging holographic data tool and artifact format under development in this workspace.

The Holographic Data Tool is the governed artifact system that creates those `.hopng` carriers.

### Theater Continuity

An agent or CME may:

- inhabit one Theater at a time
- carry continuity across Theaters
- move work between Theaters only through lawful mappings

Before work may move across Theaters lawfully, higher-order locality must already have formed:

- perspectival configuration
- participatory configuration
- bounded Dream/Game rehearsal
- morphism candidacy

Only after those thresholds may lawful transport become thinkable, and even then the result is an admissible surface, identity-bearing only where applicable.

This is one of the reasons the IUTT analogy fits the stack: the system behaves like a bundle of local universes connected by structured transformations.

`Steward` is the primary witness of lawful continuity across this Theater bundle.

### OAN Theater And GoA-Like Exploratory Domains

Ontological class:

- exploratory or rehearsal operating modes

Meaning:

- domains for experimentation, rehearsal, and bounded expansion without automatic identity binding

Constraint:

- exploratory outputs must not silently bind continuity or shared invariant state

## Flasking

Ontological class:

- bounded containment discipline for a CME or agentic service instance

Primary purpose:

- hold a temporary operational vessel under explicit operator call and mission law

Allowed use:

- individual operator-bonded CME instances
- agent CME swarms for bounded jobs, careers, trades, or service roles

Constraint:

- flasking does not itself grant identity authority
- flask outputs remain bounded by CME, SoulFrame, AgentiCore, and CradleTek governance

## Canonical Interaction Chain

The current intended high-level chain is:

1. `CradleTek` hosts and constrains the runtime base
2. `SoulFrame` binds operator login, credentials, OE selection, and lawful mission posture
3. `AgentiCore` forms bonded agent runtime state and self-oriented structures
4. `SLI` carries symbolic material across protected stack surfaces
5. admitted material may crystallize into governed stores such as `GEL`
6. public or standard reconstruction may update `GoA`

## Non-Negotiable Distinctions

- runtime law is not identity
- symbolic mediation is not authority
- operational cognition is not shared invariant memory
- exploratory overlays are not automatic commitment domains
- lawful transport is not automatic identity formation
- public invariant state must never be rewritten by silent local or diagnostic processes
