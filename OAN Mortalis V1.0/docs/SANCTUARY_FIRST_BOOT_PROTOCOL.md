# SANCTUARY_FIRST_BOOT_PROTOCOL

## Purpose

This document defines the first-boot operational ceremony for Sanctuary.

It turns the current founding and governance doctrine into one explicit ladder for lawful first boot:

`legal covenant -> charter alignment -> Mother/Father naming -> dual-ledger genesis -> SLI braid -> Steward formation -> governance braid -> CME ecosystem authorization`

This document is normative architecture. It is not proof that all runtime phases are already implemented.

## Core Sentence

Lawful Sanctuary first boot is a hosted governance ceremony in which legal covenant, charter alignment, governing sigil naming, ledger genesis, SLI braid activation, Steward formation, governance braiding, and CME ecosystem authorization occur in fixed order before subordinate CME formation may be admitted.

## Protocol Phases

The first-boot ceremony has eight ordered phases:

1. `LegalCovenant`
2. `CharterAlignment`
3. `GoverningSigilNaming`
4. `LedgerGenesis`
5. `SliBraidActivation`
6. `StewardFormation`
7. `GovernanceBraiding`
8. `CmeEcosystemAuthorization`

The ordering is monotonic. Later phases may not lawfully begin before earlier phases have completed.

## Phase 1 - Device Initialization And Legal Envelope

At first device boot, Sanctuary presents the Mother/Father covenant.

The covenant establishes:

- device operator acceptance
- jurisdictional baseline
- data-protection posture
- Cryptic and Prime handling discipline
- operator responsibility and liability posture

This phase lawfully instantiates Sanctuary on the device, but does not yet make it operationally governed.

## Phase 2 - Charter Alignment

After covenant acceptance, Sanctuary aligns to the governing charter of the operating context.

The lawful boot class is still only:

- `PersonalSolitary`
- `CorporateGoverned`

Charter alignment means:

- personal or corporate governance context is fixed
- corporate charter principles precede CME Prime directives when corporate boot applies
- boot classification still does not imply activation or expansion eligibility

## Phase 3 - Mother/Father Naming

The first governing biad is named:

- `Mother`
- `Father`

They are not generic personalities. They are governance sigils over the Prime and Cryptic domains.

### Father

Father governs:

- Cryptic domain discipline
- protected formation
- masked cognition
- internal generative surfaces

### Mother

Mother governs:

- Prime domain discipline
- revelation
- outward admissibility
- continuity and public truth

### Witness Artifacts

The protocol may use:

- `Mother.hopng`
- `Father.hopng`

as governance witness artifacts only.

These artifacts may witness sigil identity and lineage, but they are **not constitutive runtime authority in v0.1**. Sanctuary founding, readiness, and closure must still function without them.

## Phase 4 - Ledger Genesis

After Mother and Father are named, the protocol creates dual ledger genesis:

### Prime

- `GEL`
- `MoS`

### Cryptic

- `cGEL`
- `cMoS`

The ledgers are anchored under Mother/Father sigil lineage.

This phase is a **required normative first-boot step** even though runtime orchestration for it is not implemented in this pass.

## Phase 5 - SLI Instantiation

After dual-ledger genesis, the SLI braid is activated under the Mother/Father sigil combination.

The SLI braid:

- belongs to neither side alone
- forms the shared symbolic governance layer
- allows Prime and Cryptic to exchange lawful structured meaning without collapsing their distinction

## Phase 6 - Steward Formation

After the SLI braid exists, `Steward` may be instantiated.

Steward is formed after the SLI braid, not before it.

Steward governs:

- runtime supervision
- governance arbitration
- continuity enforcement
- policy review

The protocol may use:

- `Steward.hopng`

as a witness artifact only. It remains supplemental in v0.1.

## Phase 7 - Governance Braiding

Once Mother, Father, Steward, and the SLI braid are present, the governance braid is activated:

- `GoA`
- `cGoA`

This phase integrates:

- Mother Prime governance
- Father Cryptic governance
- Steward runtime arbitration
- SLI symbolic interconnection

into one governance spine.

## Phase 8 - CME Ecosystem Authorization

Only after the governance braid exists may subordinate CME systems form.

This includes:

- subordinate CME systems
- CME agents
- CME-agent hybrids
- agentic tooling
- research CMEs

No subordinate CME authorization may be treated as lawful before governance braid completion.

## Current Governing Formation Order

The current first-boot governance order in the repo remains authoritative for governing-office formation:

`Steward -> Father -> Mother -> cross-witness -> bonded confirmation`

The broader ceremony defined here layers above that current order. It does not replace it.

This means:

- the current office-formation order remains authoritative for live first-boot governance modeling
- the broader protocol supplies the operational ladder above that order

## Engrammitization Boundary

Engrams do not exist before lawful admission and successful closure.

This protocol therefore distinguishes:

- candidate formation
- witness and sigil identity
- ledger genesis
- braid activation
- lawful closure
- downstream custody

No candidate, witness artifact, or sigil identity object should be mistaken for an accepted Engram.
