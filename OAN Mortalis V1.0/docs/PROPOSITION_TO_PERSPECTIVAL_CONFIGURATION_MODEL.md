## 1. Perspectival Configuration

Perspectival configuration is the first higher-order threshold at which a bounded cognitive organism does not merely state a proposition, but holds that proposition from a situated orientation.

This means:

- proposition is not yet perspective
- procedure is not yet perspective
- perspective is not yet participation
- situated orientation is not yet identity binding

At this threshold:

- a proposition-bearing world may be held from somewhere
- a procedure-bearing world may be traversed from somewhere
- cognitive locality becomes stance-bearing without yet becoming participatory or transport-capable

## 2. Perspective Anchors

Perspectival configuration requires three anchors.

### Self-Reference

Self-reference is the first perspective anchor.

It names the lawful bounded point from which orientation occurs.

It is not yet:

- governed identity by default
- Sanctuary accountability
- permission to bind continuity across localities

### Other-Reference

Other-reference is the second perspective anchor.

It names the lawful bounded exterior presence toward which orientation may be directed.

It is not yet:

- self-collapse
- shared identity
- participatory fusion

### Relation

Relation is the lawful bridge between self-reference and other-reference.

Relation allows a world to be held as:

- oriented
- directed
- differentiated
- cognitively situated

Relation must not be treated as identity collapse.

## 3. Preserved Invariants

During perspectival configuration, the stack must preserve the proposition-bearing invariants that make later inspection and review possible.

These include at minimum:

- subject, predicate, and object polarity where the proposition surface already carries it
- qualifier and context distinctions
- bounded procedural ordering where procedure has already stabilized
- the distinction between self-reference, other-reference, and relation
- the distinction between generated structure and accepted truth

Perspectival configuration may add orientation.

It must not erase the proposition-bearing structure that made orientation possible.

## 4. False Perspective Blur And Illicit Collapse

False perspective blur occurs when the system mistakes situated orientation for stronger claims than it can lawfully support.

Forbidden collapses include:

- treating proposition as though perspective were already present
- treating perspective as though participation were already present
- treating relation as though it were identity
- treating orientation as though it were admissibility
- treating situated stance as though it were governed identity
- attempting transport before lawful inhabitation has occurred

## 5. Relationship To Later Thresholds

This document does not define:

- participatory configuration
- bounded Dream/Game rehearsal
- morphism candidacy
- lawful transport
- Sanctuary-governed accountability

Those later thresholds depend on perspectival configuration, but they are not reducible to it.
