# PRIME_CRYPTIC_DATA_TOPOLOGY

## Purpose

This document defines the Prime and Cryptic data-sovereignty topology of the active `OAN Mortalis V1.0` stack.

It exists to answer the missing architectural question behind the current `GEL` pressure:

- what data belongs to Prime
- what data belongs to Cryptic
- what crosses through SoulFrame
- what is operational only in AgentiCore
- what returns to sovereign custody on CME collapse

Assessment date:

- March 6, 2026

## Root Statement

The stack must not treat all engram-bearing data as one kind of data.

The primary fracture line is not merely project anatomy.

It is data sovereignty and lifecycle:

- Prime service plane
- Cryptic sovereign identity plane

Everything else should be derived from that split.

## Prime/Cryptic Meaning Law

Prime and Cryptic are not peer origin zones.

They form a one-way biadic relation of meaning and custody:

- Cryptic is the source domain
- Prime is the processed domain

### 1. Cryptic-First Identity Law

All genuine Engineered Cognition, identity-bearing inscription, and Engrammitization occur in the Cryptic Theater first.

Cryptic is the primary domain of:

- identity formation
- protected custody
- continuity-bearing inscription
- sovereign data practice

### 2. Prime As Derivative Law

Prime data is never primary identity substance.

Prime data is the processed, checked, pointerized, redacted, encrypted-for-release, or otherwise lawfully transformed derivative of Cryptic-held material.

### 3. Irreversibility Law

The relation is one-way in authority and disclosure:

- Cryptic may produce Prime
- Prime may not reveal Cryptic
- Prime may not reconstruct Cryptic as sovereign substance
- Prime may not modify Cryptic directly

### 4. Biadic Meaning Law

Prime and Cryptic are a biad of meaning, not a mirrored pair of equal stores.

One term is the protected source.

The other is the lawful outcome of source processing.

### 5. Non-Sovereignty Of Prime Law

Prime may:

- serve
- publish
- route
- expose
- operationalize lawful outputs

Prime may never become the sovereign author of the Cryptic state from which it was derived.

### 6. Return-Path Law

Any return toward Cryptic custody must not occur from Prime as such.

Return may occur only through a governed re-Engrammitization path in which operational residue is:

- re-evaluated
- lawfully reinscribed
- admitted under proper authority on the Cryptic side

That means write-back is not Prime modifying Cryptic.

It is a new Cryptic-side act of governed Engrammitization informed by prior operational activity.

## Doctrinal Short Form

- Cryptic engenders; Prime expresses.
- Cryptic inscribes; Prime derives.
- Cryptic may yield Prime; Prime may neither unveil nor govern Cryptic.

## Topology Summary

### Prime Service Plane

Prime-side CradleTek contains:

- `GoA`
- `GEL`
- `MoS`

These are the public-facing, redacted, encrypted-pointer, and telemetry-visible service surfaces used to conduct the hosted stack.

Prime means the data has been processed from Cryptic-origin material, checked, and either:

- encrypted with pointers
- redacted to lawful release surfaces
- or unmasked for public use under data practice

They support:

- primary Engrammitization for hosted service use
- internal agentic work
- external CME construction
- publication and operational routing
- metacognitive and governance telemetry needed to manage the stack

Prime is a service plane, not the full sovereign store of identity-bearing reality.

### Cryptic Sovereign Plane

Cryptic-side CradleTek contains:

- `cGoA`
- `cGEL`
- `cMoS`

These are protected internal services governed by Cryptic custody and SLI-mediated access.

Cryptic means the data is still live, unmasked, subject to protected data practices, and treated as the source domain of sovereign inscription.

They support:

- pre-Engrammitized processing
- protected legally retained material
- sovereign continuity-bearing self-state custody
- protected telemetry and tracking obligations
- guarded return from active CME collapse

Cryptic is not merely a darker cache of Prime.

It is the sovereign custody side of the stack.

## Service And Store Roles

### GoA

Prime `GoA` is the hosted Garden of Almost service plane for public-facing and shared operational context.

It carries:

- redacted or encrypted pointers
- service-plane operational context
- Prime telemetry access needed for lawful CME and governance operation
- processed and checked data that is lawful to expose in Prime form

It does not own private sovereign identity custody.

### cGoA

`cGoA` is the Cryptic Garden of Almost.

Its role is:

- pre-Engrammitized data processing
- cleaving data into engrams or compostable later-use stores
- protected discovery and analysis staging for Dream Theater
- live unmasked processing under data practice

It serves:

- `GoA`
- `cGEL`

It does not directly serve personalized CME identity data.

Personalized CME identity data is served through `cSelfGEL` and sovereign custody paths instead.

### GEL

Prime `GEL` is the hosted Golden Engram Library service surface.

It supports:

- hosted service-level engram collection
- primary Engrammitization
- operational publication
- public or shared-use engram-facing access under governance
- Prime-safe encrypted, redacted, pointerized, or lawfully public forms

It is not the final sovereign resting layer for protected CME self-state.

### cGEL

`cGEL` is the Cryptic Golden Engram Library.

It stores:

- legally required retained protected information
- collective CME-use protected material
- taboo or protected social surfaces
- internal-use protected data states
- live unmasked retained material still governed by data practice

It serves:

- `GEL`
- Engineered Cognition

It serves by:

- doping
- pointers
- masked or governed access

It must not unmask protected internal data without governance and policy verification.

### MoS

Prime `MoS` is the Prime-facing Mantle of Sovereigns surface.

It exposes:

- redacted
- encrypted
- pointerized
- telemetry-compatible sovereignty-facing material

It exists so the hosted Prime plane can lawfully operate without directly inheriting protected Cryptic raw stores.

### cMoS

`cMoS` is the Cryptic Mantle of Sovereigns.

It is the sovereign continuity store for:

- `cOE`
- `cSelfGEL`
- protected CME internal data stores
- raw internal protected data constructs
- legally required tracking and telemetry retention for AI and data-protection practice

It serves:

- `SelfGEL`
- Engineered Cognition directly
- Telemetry indirectly by governed pointers

It must protect masked constructs rather than expose them by operational convenience.

## SoulFrame And AgentiCore Placement

### SoulFrame

SoulFrame is the SLI-mediated transfer membrane between sovereign custody and operational use.

SoulFrame:

- provisions mitigated self-state outward from sovereign custody
- governs transfer from `cMoS` and related cryptic custody surfaces into active operational use
- governs return Engrammitization from CME collapse back into sovereign custody

SoulFrame must prevent raw operational drift from polluting sovereign continuity stores.

### AgentiCore

AgentiCore is the operational cognitive workspace.

AgentiCore:

- works on SoulFrame-mediated `SelfGEL` and `cSelfGEL`
- uses mitigated working self-state during active CME runtime
- does not own canonical identity root
- must not directly pollute sovereign custody

## CME Lifecycle

The corrected lifecycle is:

1. primary Engrammitization occurs in Prime/hosted CradleTek service surfaces under `GoA`, `GEL`, `cGoA`, and `cGEL` as appropriate
2. `OE`, `cOE`, `SelfGEL`, and `cSelfGEL` are sovereignized into `MoS` and `cMoS` according to Prime/Cryptic law
3. SoulFrame provisions mitigated self-state outward for active operation
4. AgentiCore uses that mitigated self-state for CME runtime behavior
5. on CME collapse, governed return Engrammitization flows back through SoulFrame into sovereign custody

This return path must be:

- governed
- narrowed
- continuity-aware
- non-dump-like

## Idle State Law

When no CME instances are active and no SoulFrame or AgentiCore surfaces are using the stores:

- the database layer is idle as service
- extended CME-local data structures are not assumed to be ambiently active
- governing agent surfaces such as `Mother`, `Father`, and `Steward` behave as operator-bound CME-like stack instances, not as permanently expanded raw-state blobs

Extended stack structures should therefore be treated as active-stack phenomena, not as mandatory idle-state collection.

## Refactor Implication

The next major refactor should not treat “storage” as one category.

It should distinguish at least:

- Prime hosted service storage
- Cryptic sovereign custody storage
- SoulFrame transfer and membrane objects
- AgentiCore working-state objects
- collapse return and continuity ingestion paths

This topology should guide the next code split after `GEL.Contracts`.
