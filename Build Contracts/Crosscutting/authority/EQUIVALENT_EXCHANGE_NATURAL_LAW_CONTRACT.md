# Equivalent Exchange Natural Law Contract

## Purpose

This contract fixes the active build reading for the Law of Equivalent Exchange
as an `SLI` / Lisp natural law.

It is not a claim that the current runtime already exposes one finished
operator that computes this law in full.

It is the authority note that names the governing relation the line should be
preparing to answer.

## Core Read

Equivalent exchange in this stack does not mean:

- equal value returned for equal value spent
- visual sameness across transformation
- simple symmetry of input and output

The stronger read is:

`equivalent exchange = burden-preserving, continuity-safe transformation under admissibility`

So the active law is:

- admissibility decides whether transformation may proceed at all
- equivalence decides whether governing burden was preserved
- recovery decides whether lawful identity remains one after distribution
- refusal or cleaving are truthful outcomes when those conditions fail

## Admissibility And Equivalence

The line must keep these questions separate.

Admissibility asks:

- may this structure enter, move, act, or carry forward under the current law
  surface

Equivalence asks:

- did the resulting transformation preserve the governing burden of the source
  structure

So the current invariants are:

- admissible does not equal equivalent
- lawful does not equal sufficient
- reachable does not equal repayable

## Recovery, Unity, And Cleaving

The current working formula is:

`Cleave exactly where lawful recovery fails, and unify exactly where invariant recovery holds.`

That means:

- a structure remains one only when admissibility, burden, and continuity stay
  under the same governing relation through distribution and recovery
- a structure must cleave when distribution reveals incompatible admissibility,
  burden, or continuity classes
- a structure must be refused or deferred when it does not lawfully enter the
  surface at all

The build must therefore treat refusal and cleaving as truthful outcomes, not
as mere error states or missing implementation.

## Burden Families

The current burden families are:

- `constitutional_burden`
- `body_burden`
- `relational_burden`
- `cognitive_burden`
- `continuity_burden`

These are doctrine-bearing distinctions first.

When the runtime later takes them up, it must do so in narrower, receipted,
telemetry-backed, contract-backed forms rather than collapsing them into one
generic scalar.

## SLI And Lisp Read

This law is treated here as an `SLI` / Lisp natural law because:

- `SLI` carries continuity-bearing structure
- Lisp or a Lisp-like symbolic medium carries compositional interior
  transformation
- equivalent exchange names what transformations owe across that continuity and
  composition field

So the build-facing read is not:

- English says one thing and Lisp says another

It is:

- witnessed ingress must enter lawfully
- symbolic interior transformation must preserve burden and continuity
- outward return must not counterfeit unity or certainty

This keeps the law aligned with the `HITL -> SLI` bridge rather than reducing
it to a language-translation slogan.

## Chapter-Five Relation

Chapter five is one of the first major bonded corpus chambers where this law
becomes legible in operator-facing form.

It does that by making protocol, covenant, calibration, consent, rupture,
return, and seal behave as burden-bearing transitions rather than as decorative
theme.

So for the active build hold, chapter five should be read as a major teaching
carrier of this law, but not as proof that the runtime already computes every
part of it.

## Build Restraint

For the current line:

- this law is admitted as crosscutting authority and build-shaping doctrine
- it may inform documentation, contracts, tests, and later runtime design
- it must not be overclaimed as a shipped universal primitive until narrower
  operator families, receipts, telemetry, and audit surfaces actually exist

Candidate future runtime-facing shapes may include:

- admissibility operator families
- burden signatures
- equivalence reviews
- recovery reviews
- governed cleaving decisions

But those remain future narrowing work until build truth catches up.

## Working Summary

The current law is:

- admit by law
- judge by burden
- recover by continuity
- unify where recovery holds
- cleave or refuse where it fails

That is the active build reading of equivalent exchange as an `SLI` / Lisp
natural law.
