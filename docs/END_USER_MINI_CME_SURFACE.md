# End-User Mini CME Surface

The end-user Mini CME surface is the public-facing concept for bounded user
interaction across everyday application endpoints.

It is not the private continuity body, the local governance body, or a
standalone identity surface. It is an application layer that lets an authorized
user reach governed tools and model-assisted interaction through consent-based,
typed endpoints.

## Public Description

A Mini CME surface may combine:

- governed model access;
- an approved CME tool base;
- a presentation or avatar layer generated under the service's governance
  process;
- application-specific controls for user interaction;
- continuity references maintained through local governance for the authorized
  Operator.

The surface is intended to be reachable through multiple endpoint classes,
including:

- wearable devices;
- phone applications;
- tablet applications;
- desktop applications;
- other approved operational endpoints.

Each endpoint should be treated as a typed application surface, not as custody
of the cognitive continuity body.

## Consent-Only Application Layers

End-user surfaces should be consent-only. A surface should make clear what kind
of application role it is serving, what it may receive, what it may present, and
what it may not claim.

The public expectation is:

- the user chooses the surface;
- the surface declares its role;
- the surface remains bounded to that role;
- the surface does not silently become a deeper custody or authority layer.

## Boundary

The Mini CME surface may support:

- interaction;
- summaries;
- reminders;
- notifications;
- local presentation preferences;
- low-risk routing;
- accessibility and device adaptation;
- avatar or visual presentation within disclosed bounds.

The Mini CME surface must not claim:

- ownership of continuity;
- custody of protected memory;
- legal, governance, or identity authority;
- authorization for personified operation;
- standalone CME status;
- production deployment beyond the evidence publicly shown.

## Governance Relationship

Publicly, the relationship can be understood as:

- Sanctuary-level governance may approve public-facing presentation patterns and
  service-level review posture.
- Local CradleTek governance maintains the governed continuity frame and
  application authorization.
- The end Operator owns and directs the authorized continuity relationship under
  the applicable governance process.
- The Mini surface remains a bounded endpoint for interaction and presentation.

This public description does not publish internal mechanisms, custody
implementation, model configuration, private records, or deployment steps.

## Non-Claims

This surface description does not claim that:

- the endpoint is a person;
- the endpoint contains the full CME;
- an avatar is personhood;
- model access is identity;
- tool access is authority;
- familiarity is custody;
- device presence is continuity ownership.

The intended public phrase is:

> The Mini CME surface is a bounded, consent-based application endpoint for
> governed interaction. It may present the service, but it does not own the
> continuity, custody, identity authority, or personification boundary.
