# REVALIDATION LAW

## Purpose

This note governs continued validity across time for any structure, state,
permission, or outcome carried through the `Cryptic Continuity Medium`
(`CCM`), ensuring that continuity remains lawful under Prime invariants
(`pOE`) and current `TAB` conditions without collapsing into either:

- amnesia
- unauthorized persistence

## Core Principle

> Continuity is not self-validating.
> Any carried structure remains admissible only insofar as it remains valid
> under present conditions.

Continuity may carry forward structure and evidence, but current permission
must always be re-discerned when conditions demand it.

## Definitions

- `Continuity`
  - a structure, state, or outcome preserved across turns via `CCM`
- `Validity`
  - present admissibility under Prime invariants and current `TAB` conditions
- `Revalidation`
  - re-discernment to determine whether carried continuity remains valid for
    present participation
- `Evidence`
  - prior truth that may inform present discernment but does not itself grant
    present permission
- `Permission`
  - current admissibility to act, persist, or participate

## Law I - Admissible Persistence

A continuity may persist without immediate revalidation only if:

- domain conditions remain unchanged
- role standing remains unchanged
- constraint context remains unchanged
- no temporal or causal pressure has altered admissibility
- no conflict or challenge has been introduced

If any of the above is not guaranteed, persistence must degrade to
evidence-only until revalidated.

## Law II - Revalidation Triggers

Revalidation is required upon any of the following:

1. `DomainShift`
   - crossing into a new domain or sub-domain
   - material change inside the current domain
2. `RoleShiftOrAmbiguity`
   - role changes, expands, or becomes unclear
3. `ConstraintChange`
   - new constraints introduced
   - prior constraints modified or removed
4. `TemporalPressure`
   - elapsed time exceeds declared or implied validity window
   - freshness materially affects correctness or legitimacy
5. `ConflictOrChallenge`
   - contradiction introduced
   - evidence disputed
   - outcome contested
6. `ActionEscalation`
   - increased consequence or scope relative to original affirmation

Upon trigger, prior continuity must be treated as evidence, not permission.

## Law III - Evidence Versus Permission

> Prior truth travels as evidence, not as present permission.

Evidence may:

- inform re-discernment
- accelerate convergence
- reduce redundancy

Evidence may not:

- authorize action
- bypass threshold
- grant standing in a new or altered context

Only re-affirmed outcomes may act as current permission.

## Law IV - Continuity Preservation

Revalidation must not collapse continuity unnecessarily.

Therefore:

- revalidation operates on admissibility, not existence
- prior structures remain in `CCM` as trace and evidence
- only permission state is reset, not historical continuity

This prevents:

- loss of learning or context
- forced amnesia
- collapse of cryptic structure into dead storage

## Law V - Interaction With `TAB` States

### `Proceed`

- requires current valid permission
- if triggered, must revalidate before proceeding

### `Hold`

- must include explicit revalidation condition
- must include temporal or contextual trigger
- cannot persist beyond its revalidation condition

### `Refuse`

- remains valid as a recorded exclusion
- may be reopened only through explicit revalidation

### `Escalate`

- transfers resolution authority
- does not erase origin trace
- receiving domain or authority must revalidate independently

## Law VI - Responsibility Continuity

Revalidation must preserve responsibility trace:

- original authorship and basis remain attached as evidence
- revalidation produces a new accountable state rather than a silent overwrite
- no outcome may erase prior responsibility, merge authorship ambiguously, or
  obscure basis of change

## Law VII - Anti Persistence Guards

The system must prevent:

- stale truth acting as present authority
- permission carry without re-discernment
- implicit validity by duration
- continuity used to bypass threshold
- unchallenged carry across domain boundaries

## Summary

> `TAB` governs lawful transition.
> `CCM` governs lawful continuity.
> `REVALIDATION` governs lawful continued validity across time.

Together they ensure:

- nothing unlawful may occur
- nothing meaningful collapses or drifts
- nothing persists beyond its rightful validity

## Minimal Test Questions

For any carried structure:

- what was originally affirmed
- under what conditions was it valid
- what has changed since
- does it still meet admissibility now
- if not, what must be re-discerned

If these cannot be answered, revalidation is required.

## Closing Line

> Continuity may remember, but only revalidation may authorize.
