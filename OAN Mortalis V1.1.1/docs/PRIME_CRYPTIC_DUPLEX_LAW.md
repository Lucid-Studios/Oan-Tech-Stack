# PRIME CRYPTIC DUPLEX LAW

## Purpose

This note defines the lawful split between `Prime` and `Cryptic` surfaces in
the active `V1.1.1` line.

It exists to keep five things distinct:

- readable and witnessable canonical surfaces
- hot execution-bearing surfaces
- recognition versus instantiation
- candidate return versus admitted promotion
- auditable duplex transit versus silent mutation

This is a duplex-law note.
It is not proof that every downstream runtime office already stands in code.

## Core Definition

The current stack should read `Prime` and `Cryptic` as one duplexed field with
different offices:

- `Prime` surfaces are readable, witnessable, canonical, and publication-safe
- `Cryptic` `c*` surfaces are hot, encrypted, mutable, and execution-bearing

In compact form:

> `Prime` recognizes and witnesses. `Cryptic` instantiates and works.

The active `V1.1.1` read may now also be stated as a membrane relation:

- `Prime Membrane` is the lawful source surface of readable presence
- `Cryptic` is the duplex-projected shadow field where cognition may remain
  live before closure

That membrane clarification does not replace the duplex law.
It narrows how the repo should read packet and handoff work in this area.

## Prime Surfaces

`Prime` surfaces are the readable and supervisory side of the duplex.

Their office is to:

- recognize verified standing
- witness canonical state
- hold publication-safe form
- support audit and review
- provide recovery-facing and operator-readable continuity

They are not the hot execution lane.

Typical examples in the current doctrinal read are:

- `MoS`
- readable `GoA` or shared canonical surfaces
- publication-safe witness packets
- Prime-side hosted and review-facing posture surfaces

## Cryptic Surfaces

`Cryptic` surfaces are the hot and execution-bearing side of the duplex.

Their office is to:

- instantiate protected working form
- carry mutable session state
- host live symbolic and cognition-bearing work
- emit candidate returns
- remain sealed until audited

Typical examples in the current doctrinal read are:

- `cMoS`
- `cGoA`
- `cOE`
- `cSelfGEL`
- `c*` receipts and live work ledgers

## Non-Collapse Rule

The duplex must not collapse in either direction.

### Prime must not silently mutate from hot work

The stack must not treat:

- live execution
- coherent output
- session continuity
- repeated usage

as sufficient to rewrite canonical `Prime` surfaces.

### Cryptic must not be mistaken for canon

The stack must not treat:

- a hot working session
- protected internal state
- live posture modulation
- temporary repair or calibration

as already-admitted public or canonical truth.

## Duplex Transit Rule

The lawful relation is:

- `Prime -> Cryptic`
  recognition, authorization, templating, and bounded instantiation
- `Cryptic -> Prime`
  candidate return, audit, and possible promotion

That means the duplex is not a mirror.
It is a governed transit path with asymmetry.

`Prime` does not simply become `Cryptic`.
`Cryptic` does not simply write back into `Prime`.

## Operational Rule

Native `SLI` work under live `EC` belongs only in the `Cryptic` lane.

That means:

- the hot symbolic web is a cryptic working surface
- live `AgentiCore` cognition occurs in cryptic office space
- modulation, repair, and working posture remain cryptic until reviewed

The readable and canonical side may witness those facts, but it should not
become the place where hot execution silently persists itself.

## Authority Placement

Authority is split by office.

- issuance authority stays above the hot runtime lane
- operative commit capacity lives in issued cryptic office such as `cOE`
- persistence authority stays above the hot lane
- promotion authority remains audit-bound

So the duplex law denies two common errors:

- all authority is outside runtime
- all authority is inside runtime

The lawful read is narrower:

> cryptic runtime may work under issued office, but canon and durable
> persistence still require authority above that hot lane.

The first bounded `EC` install-to-first-Prime state now composes this duplex
path without widening it: Cryptic sensor-body work may reach stable-one
discernment, and Prime may receive retained standing, while Mother/Father
domain-role application, cradle-local governing surface selection, and Prime
closure remain withheld.

## Immediate Build Consequence

Any future file, receipt, or runtime seam in this area should answer:

1. is this a `Prime` surface or a `Cryptic` surface
2. is it recognition, instantiation, candidate return, or promotion
3. what authority is still outside it

If a seam cannot answer those questions plainly, it should be narrowed or held
before promotion.
