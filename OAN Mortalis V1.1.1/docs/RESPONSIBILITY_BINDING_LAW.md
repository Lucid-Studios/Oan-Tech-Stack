# RESPONSIBILITY BINDING LAW

## Status

Proposed - Anchor Law Candidate

## Purpose

This note defines how responsibility is attached, preserved, and transferred
across lawful outcomes inside the active `OAN Mortalis V1.1.1` cognitive body
so that:

- no action occurs without an accountable bearer
- no refusal occurs without attributable judgment
- no hold exists without stewardship
- no escalation dissolves responsibility
- no outcome may be rewritten without trace

## Core Law

> No lawful outcome may exist without an attributable bearer of consequence.

## Binding Principle

> Permission to resolve must bind responsibility to the resolution.

## Responsibility Of `Proceed`

Any transition to `Proceed` must bind:

- a primary bearer
- a scope of action
- a basis of discernment

The primary bearer shall:

- be accountable for downstream consequence within the defined scope
- not defer responsibility to prior emergence, ambient context, or shared
  atmosphere

Plural affirmation may exist, but one or more explicitly identified bearers
must exist.

Compressed:

> if it proceeds, someone stands behind it

## Responsibility Of `Refuse`

Any transition to `Refuse` must bind:

- a refusal authority
- a basis of insufficiency or contradiction
- a closure condition preventing silent re-entry

The refusing bearer shall:

- be accountable for the exclusion
- not allow silent reintroduction without explicit re-evaluation

## Stewardship Of `Hold`

While in `Hold`, responsibility shall not be absent.

A `Hold` must bind:

- a steward
- a tracked uncertainty
- a reevaluation obligation

The steward shall:

- maintain visibility of the hold
- ensure reevaluation occurs
- bear cost accountability for continued suspension

Compressed:

> holding is not neutral; it is actively maintained

## Responsibility In `Escalate`

Escalation must:

- transfer resolution authority
- preserve originating responsibility trace

Escalation shall bind:

- an originating node
- a receiving authority

Responsibility must not:

- be erased
- be anonymized
- be collapsed into system-level abstraction

Compressed:

> escalation transfers obligation to decide, not responsibility for origin

## Plural Participation Binding

When multiple surfaces contribute to an outcome:

- each contributing surface must be identifiable
- each contribution must be classified as affirming, dissenting, or holding

The outcome must define one of the following:

- a primary bearer with supporting contributors
- explicitly shared responsibility with defined scope per participant

Plurality must not result in:

- diluted responsibility
- indistinguishable authorship
- post-hoc ambiguity

## Receipt Requirement

Every resolved outcome must produce a receipt containing:

- outcome type
- responsible bearer or bearers
- basis of decision
- timestamp or sequence position
- scope and constraints

Receipts must:

- be immutable
- be auditable
- prevent retroactive reassignment of responsibility

Compressed:

> what was permitted must remain attributable

## Anti Diffusion Invariant

The system must not allow:

- responsibility to be distributed without attribution
- responsibility to be deferred to "the system"
- responsibility to be erased through escalation or time

If an outcome exists, someone, or a defined set, must stand behind it.

## Temporal Persistence Of Responsibility

Responsibility shall:

- persist as long as the outcome has effect
- remain linked even if surrounding context evolves

Revalidation may alter future responsibility, but past responsibility must
remain intact.

## Participation Boundary

Only responsibly bound outcomes may:

- participate in action
- influence downstream state
- persist as valid system structure

Unbound or ambiguously bound outcomes must not:

- propagate
- be treated as authoritative
- influence decision-making

## Completion Posture

This law completes the consequence side of the transition seam.

The system is considered structurally ready at this seam when every outcome
can be traced to a bearer who permitted it to stand.

That means:

- no movement without ownership
- no waiting without stewardship
- no refusal without accountable judgment

## Minimal Form

> If it proceeds, someone stands behind it.
> If it refuses, someone stands behind that.
> If it holds, someone is maintaining it.
> If it escalates, responsibility does not disappear.
>
> Nothing becomes real without someone bearing it.
