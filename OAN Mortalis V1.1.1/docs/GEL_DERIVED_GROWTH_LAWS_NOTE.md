## First Derived Laws

### L1 Branch Growth

A branch may grow from a trunk only when it remains anchored to that trunk and
does not redefine it.

### L2 Branch Purity

A branch must remain lawful to its own layer under ongoing residency
discernment.

### L3 Pruning

An unlawful retained branch must be decomposed, rerouted, or refused, not kept
as corrupted retention.

### L4 Grove Relation

Distinct trunks may relate without identity collapse.

### L5 Anchor Merge Refusal

Distinct anchors do not merge by overlap alone.

### L6 Domain Separation

Future groves remain modular across domains and do not overwrite each other.

### L7 Translation Admissibility

Not every object is ready for translation. Only layer-clean, anchor-stable,
dependency-safe objects may pass.

### L8 Posture-Gated Procedure

Procedural growth is lawful only when posture and lower-layer grounding are
already stable.

## Current Seating

`L1` through `L5` are contract-backed now.

`L6` through `L8` are doctrine-only now.

All first derived laws are subordinate to `A0` and must preserve it.

`maybe` remains the existing repo-native provisional standing and is not a new
