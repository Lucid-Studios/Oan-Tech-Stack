# ACTION THRESHOLD LAW

## Status

Proposed - Anchor Law Candidate

## Purpose

This note defines the lawful transition from discernment into consequence
inside the active `OAN Mortalis V1.1.1` cognitive body so that:

- action may proceed without false unanimity
- partial convergence does not become silent coercion
- lawful movement remains accountable to explicit sufficiency
- waiting does not impersonate integrity when consequence is already due

## Core Law

> Action does not require total agreement, but it must require accountable
> sufficiency.

## Action Threshold Problem

The system cannot require universal convergence before every action.
If it did, lawful movement would collapse into paralysis.

The system also cannot treat any local preference, urgency claim, or
non-opposition as sufficient by itself.
If it did, action would collapse into silent coercion.

This law therefore seats the lawful middle:

- total agreement is not required
- accountable sufficiency is required

## No Total Agreement Requirement

Action shall not require every available witness, lane, or discernment surface
to produce the same outcome.

That means:

- `Affirm` from one surface does not require mirrored `Affirm` from all others
- `Hold` from one surface does not automatically veto all movement
- `Refuse` from one surface must be evaluated for scope, authority, and burden
  rather than flattened into ambient shutdown

Compressed:

> unanimity is not the threshold of lawful action

## Accountable Sufficiency Requirement

Action may proceed only when accountable sufficiency is present.

Accountable sufficiency requires all of the following:

- the action basis is explicit
- the acting scope is explicit
- the governing domain and role posture are explicit
- the live holds and refusals have been read rather than ignored
- the remaining risk, burden, and uncertainty are named in the receipt
- at least one accountable surface bears the consequence of movement

Accountable sufficiency is not:

- enthusiasm
- convenience
- urgency rhetoric
- "this is obvious"
- "this is standard"
- silence from neighboring surfaces

## Threshold Outcomes

Discernment of action readiness must resolve to exactly one of the following
threshold outcomes:

- `Proceed`
- `Hold`
- `Refuse`
- `Escalate`

These are movement permissions, not sentiment labels.

### `Proceed`

`Proceed` permits bounded action.

`Proceed` requires:

- accountable sufficiency
- an explicit actor or accountable acting surface
- a traceable basis for why movement is lawful now
- explicit note of unresolved but tolerated uncertainty

### `Hold`

`Hold` preserves provisional non-movement.

`Hold` is lawful when:

- accountable sufficiency is not yet present
- a missing witness or unresolved burden still matters materially
- movement would overclaim present standing

`Hold` does not grant innocence by duration alone.

### `Refuse`

`Refuse` blocks action.

`Refuse` is lawful when:

- the proposed action exceeds admitted standing
- the proposed action breaches domain, role, or continuity bounds
- the required witness basis is absent or contradicted at binding scope

### `Escalate`

`Escalate` routes the decision to a higher or differently scoped accountable
surface.

`Escalate` is lawful when:

- the consequence is real but local authority is insufficient
- the conflict cannot be resolved inside the current acting scope
- action would otherwise proceed under ambiguity about who may bear it

## Partial Convergence Rule

Partial convergence may lawfully support action when:

- one or more relevant surfaces affirm movement
- no binding-scope refusal remains unanswered
- all remaining holds are receipted as bounded non-readiness rather than
  silent opposition
- the accountable acting surface explicitly accepts the cost of moving before
  unanimity

Partial convergence must never be rewritten after the fact as full agreement.

Compressed:

> partial convergence may move; it may not masquerade as consensus

## No Silent Coercion Rule

The system must not manufacture action by:

- treating missing response as agreement
- treating fatigue as consent
- treating unresolved hold as non-event
- treating repeated pressure as discernment
- treating local role familiarity as universal permission

No action receipt is lawful if opposing or unresolved surfaces are simply
erased from the account.

## Urgency And Obviousness Constraint

Urgency, obviousness, familiarity, or standard practice may influence priority.
They do not by themselves satisfy the action threshold.

That means:

- urgent work still requires accountable sufficiency
- routine work still requires accountable sufficiency
- repeated work still requires accountable sufficiency

Compressed:

> urgency can accelerate review; it cannot replace lawful threshold

## Threshold Receipt Requirement

Every action-threshold decision must emit a receipt that names:

- proposed action
- acting domain
- acting role or accountable surface
- discernment basis
- live `Affirm`, `Hold`, `Refuse`, or `Escalate` posture
- unresolved burden accepted at movement time
- why accountable sufficiency was or was not met

If the outcome is `Proceed`, the receipt must also name who bears the
consequence of movement at the present scope.

## Participation Boundary

Only actions that have crossed a lawful threshold may:

- influence downstream operational state
- claim they were admitted to move
- bind later surfaces to incurred consequence

Held, refused, or merely emergent action candidates must not:

- leak into execution paths
- inherit the standing of a lawful action
- be retroactively described as already admitted

## Anti Paralysis Invariant

The system must not:

- confuse caution with virtue
- confuse unresolvedness with neutrality
- confuse indefinite waiting with integrity

A held action still spends time, memory, attention, and trust.
Hold is therefore a bounded non-movement posture, not a moral exemption.

## Completion Posture

This law completes the transition question between discernment and
consequence.

The system is considered structurally ready at this seam when it can:

- permit action without requiring unanimity
- withhold action without pretending withholding is costless
- escalate when local authority is insufficient
- record who moved, why, and at what unresolved cost

## Minimal Form

> Do not wait for impossible unanimity.
> Do not move on convenience.
> Let action cross only under accountable sufficiency.
> Let hold remain lawful, but never costless.
> Let refusal bind where standing is not present.
> Let escalation name the missing authority.

## Notes

- this law governs movement, not ontology
- this law is subordinate to bounded discernment
- this law prepares the seam for later hold-resolution and
