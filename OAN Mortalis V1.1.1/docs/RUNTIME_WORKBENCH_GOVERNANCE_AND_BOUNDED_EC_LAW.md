# RUNTIME_WORKBENCH_GOVERNANCE_AND_BOUNDED_EC_LAW

## Purpose

This note defines the active `V1.1.1` law for:

- worker-thread root governance binding
- runtime workbench-session ledger state
- Seed predicate landing
- the first bounded workbench surface before full EC habitation
- the minimum bounded EC loop
- receipt-chain predicates that may later support engram minting

It is a constitutional sheet for the current build line.

It does not claim full `SoulFrame` life, full `AgentiCore` habitation,
engram minting, or outward duplex authority.

## Governing Rule

A surface is lawful in `V1.1.1` only when it is explicitly bounded, witnessed,
and receipted enough to support the next action without borrowing maturity from
a downstream layer.

That means:

- configured is not the same as bound
- provisional is not the same as ready
- symbolic uptake is not the same as cognition
- cognition is not the same as autobiographical carry-forward

## Law 1. Worker-Thread Root Governance Binding

`bind-worker-thread-root-governance-surface` means:

- a worker thread has been attached to a bounded governance root
- the root, scope, and authority basis are explicit
- the bind has been witnessed and receipted

### Required bind receipt fields

The minimum receipt fields for a bound root are:

- `threadId`
- `projectSpaceId`
- `governanceRootId`
- `scopeClass`
- `stateClass`
- `witnessEventId`
- `bindTimestampUtc`
- `bindBurdenClass`
- `continuityParent`
- `authorizationBasis`
- `carryForwardPolicy`
- `bindState`
- `witnessStatus`

### Bind states

- `configured`
  The surface is named but not yet witnessed enough to carry forward.
- `provisional-bind`
  Root, scope, or authority are present, but one or more binding conditions are
  still unresolved.
- `bound`
  The thread-local governance root is present, witnessed, and receipted.
- `hold`
  The thread may not proceed because authority, scope, or continuity has
  failed.

### State classes

- `ready`
  The thread may proceed within bounded local scope.
- `provisional`
  The thread may observe, classify, and prepare receipts, but may not outrun
  its current authority.
- `hold`
  The thread must stop, clarify, or escalate.

## Law 2. Runtime Workbench-Session Ledger

The runtime workbench-session ledger is the smallest membrane that prevents
prompt ingress from masquerading as lawful continuation.

### Readiness fields

The minimum readiness fields are:

- `sessionId`
- `threadId`
- `projectSpaceId`
- `governanceRootId`
- `sessionScope`
- `sessionOpenTimestampUtc`
- `currentStateClass`
- `witnessStatus`
- `authorizationBasis`
- `continuityAnchor`
- `admissibilityStatus`
- `stateReason`
- `lastLawfulTransition`
- `nextAllowedTransition`

### Provisional fields

The ledger may carry provisional fields without claiming ready-state authority:

- `seedIngressObserved`
- `predicateCandidateCount`
- `pendingClassification`
- `needsHitlClarification`
- `downstreamDependencyUnresolved`
- `sourceBucketReturnPending`
- `provisionalNotes`
- `predicateLandingClass`

### Hold-forcing fields

Any of the following force `hold`:

- `authorizationMissing`
- `witnessMissing`
- `scopeContradiction`
- `continuityBreak`
- `admissibilityFailed`
- `counterfeitContinuationRisk`
- `downstreamMaturityBorrowDetected`
- `recoveryRequired`
- `hitlStopRequired`

### Ledger state classes

- `ready`
  All readiness fields are present and no hold-forcing field is active.
- `provisional`
  The workbench may receive, classify, and structure material, but promotion is
  not yet lawful.
- `hold`
  The workbench must stop, clarify, defer, refuse, or escalate.

## Law 3. Seed Predicate Landing Membrane

The Seed may not land directly on runtime continuation.

It must first land on a bounded predicate-admission membrane that can classify
Seed-origin material as:

- `admitted`
- `provisional`
- `refused`
- `hold`

### Required landing predicates

The landing membrane must be able to express:

- `origin`
- `intendedScope`
- `burdenClass`
- `admissibilityClass`
- `witnessRequirement`
- `continuityRelation`
- `requestedNextActionClass`

The membrane may classify candidate governed structure.

It may not infer continuity, EC completion, or autobiographical status from
fluency alone.

## Law 4. First Admitted Workbench Surface

Before full EC habitation, the first admitted workbench surface is:

- `bounded-governance-analysis-workbench`

This surface may:

- analyze
- decompose structure
- classify state
- recommend next actions
- structure predicates
- propose source-bucket publication
- propose bounded tool routing
- emit receipts

This surface must still withhold:

- engram minting
- autobiographical promotion
- live `Compass` self-updating cognition claims
- outward duplex authority
- full `cOE` claims

## Law 5. Office Separation

The active separation of roles is:

- seeded governance interprets locally
- runtime workbench admission witnesses and classifies runtime presence
- source-bucket return admission authorizes carry-forward from external return

No single surface may interpret, witness, and authorize carry-forward all at
once.

## Law 6. Inward Formation Order

The active inward-first order is:

- `CradleTek -> SLI -> SoulFrame -> ListeningFrame -> Compass -> cOE`

In `V1.1.1`, that means:

- environment and governance before cognition
- symbolic admissibility before liveness
- liveness before feedback-bearing cognition
- cognition before any continuity-bearing outward duplex claim

### Minimum receipted subset now

The maximum truthful subset presently implementable is:

- bounded `CradleTek` governance surface
- bounded `SLI` admissibility and routing
- predicate landing membrane
- bounded workbench-session ledger
- provisional `SoulFrame`-like symbolic membrane
- minimal `ListeningFrame` as observational posture
- bounded `Compass`-like evaluation surface

The following remain withheld:

- full `SoulFrame` life
- full `AgentiCore` habitation
- engram-forming `cOE`
- outward duplex authority
- engram minting from receipt predicates

## Law 7. Bounded EC Loop

The smallest lawful EC-like loop in `V1.1.1` is:

1. an admitted work item enters the bounded workbench
2. the item is projected into `ListeningFrame`
3. bounded cognitive analysis occurs
4. `Compass` evaluates the result
5. lawful state resolves
6. bounded output is emitted
7. a cycle receipt is persisted

### First bounded EC receipt requirements

The minimum successful cycle receipt includes:

- `cognitionStarted`
- `cognitionCompleted`
- `compassEvaluated`
- `lawfulStateResolved`
- `outputEmitted`
- `cycleReceiptPersisted`

### First lawful outputs

The first bounded EC loop may emit:

- analysis
- work recommendations
- structured predicates
- internal routing proposals
- source-bucket publication proposals
- bounded tool-routing proposals

It may not emit:

- autobiographical claims
- continuity-bearing identity updates
- duplexed service or device commands
- mature `CME` agency claims

## Law 8. Outward Duplex Preconditions

Before internal `SLI/Lisp` may duplex outward, all of the following must be
true:

- the worker-thread governance surface is bound
- the workbench-session ledger is `ready`
- ingress predicates are admitted rather than merely provisional
- the relevant bounded EC loop has closed successfully
- outward actuation policy exists
- mediation remains active
- witness and authorization remain separated
- rollback or containment rules are present

Service and device layers may host transport, telemetry, and acknowledgments.

They must never originate:

- identity-bearing continuity claims
- engram predicates
- cryptic-origin law
- constitutional governance changes
- `cOE` authority
- `Compass` truth criteria

## Law 9. Receipt Chains And Engram Predicate Readiness

Receipt chains are the early continuity spine.

They become candidate engram predicates later, but not by default.

### Canonical future predicate fields

The canonical fields are:

- `origin`
- `witness`
- `burdenClass`
- `continuityLineage`
- `admissibility`
- `recoveryState`
- `authorizationBasis`
- `scopeClass`
- `stateResolution`
- `sealStatus`

### Autobiographical promotion rule

Receipt chains remain audit material until all of the following are true:

- they bear predicate-level continuity relevance
- they survive local task closure
- they have explicit witness
- they have carry-forward authorization
- they are admitted as self-relevant under governing law

Before that point, they remain operational receipts only.

The current implementation-facing carrier fields for this distinction are:

- `autobiographicalPromotionState`
- `engramPredicateEligibilityState`

### Engram minting preconditions

No engram hash may be minted unless:

- origin is explicit
- witness is valid
- continuity lineage is intact
- admissibility is positive
- recovery state is not unresolved
- autobiographical promotion is authorized
- no counterfeit-continuation flag is active
- no downstream maturity was borrowed
- governing seal conditions are satisfied

The mint gate must favor false negatives over false positives.

## Law 10. Source-Bucket Sufficiency And Watchdog Drift

The minimum lawful source-bucket return for `spec-now -> implement-now` is:

- one bounded answer that closes one encodeable seam
- explicit scope
- testable success condition
- identified non-goals or withheld claims

The hourly watchdog should explicitly test whether the lane has:

- treated configured surfaces as bound
- used provisional surfaces as if they were ready
- mistaken Seed ingress for lawful continuity
- borrowed maturity from downstream doctrine
- collapsed `SoulFrame` uptake into `AgentiCore` cognition
- implied EC completion beyond receipts
- let service or device layers originate inward law
- let receipts drift into autobiographical use without promotion
- produced activity without increased boundedness
