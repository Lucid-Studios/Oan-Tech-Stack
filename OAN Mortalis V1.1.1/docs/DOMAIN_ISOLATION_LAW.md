# DOMAIN ISOLATION LAW

## Status

Proposed - Anchor Law Candidate

## Purpose

This note defines how lawful standing, admission, and consequence remain bound
to domain so that:

- legitimacy does not drift across context by implication
- affirmed structures do not borrow standing from neighboring domains
- action remains scoped to the domain that admitted it
- cross-domain transfer always requires re-discernment

## Core Law

> No structure may carry legitimacy across domains without re-discernment.

## Binding Principle

> Domain admission is local standing, not portable authority.

## Domain Locality Requirement

Any affirmed structure, admitted role, witness receipt, or action permission
stands only within the domain that lawfully admitted it.

That means:

- domain standing is local
- role standing is local
- action permission is local
- accountability remains local until explicitly transferred

Compressed:

> lawful in one domain is not lawful everywhere

## No Borrowed Legitimacy Rule

The system must not allow a structure that was:

- affirmed in `Domain A`
- admitted in `Domain A`
- witnessed in `Domain A`
- or executed in `Domain A`

to be treated as already legitimate in `Domain B` without explicit
re-discernment.

The following are not enough to justify transfer:

- semantic resemblance
- shared operator
- shared history
- prior success elsewhere
- convenience

## Cross Domain Transfer Requirement

Any attempted movement from one domain into another must pass through a
cross-domain transfer decision.

That decision must bind:

- originating domain
- candidate receiving domain
- structure or action being transferred
- current standing basis
- reason the transfer is being requested
- explicit re-discernment outcome

The transfer outcome must be exactly one of:

- `Admit`
- `Hold`
- `Refuse`
- `Escalate`

## Re-Discernment Requirement

Cross-domain transfer always requires re-discernment.

Re-discernment must ask:

- does the receiving domain recognize the prior basis as relevant evidence
- does the receiving domain impose different constraints or exclusions
- does the transfer exceed the original scope of action or authority
- would the transfer create false continuity or borrowed legitimacy

Prior lawful standing may be used as evidence.
It may not be used as automatic permission.

Compressed:

> prior admission may inform; it may not substitute

## Domain Specific Responsibility

Responsibility remains bound to the originating domain until a receiving
domain explicitly accepts scope and consequence.

That means:

- origin responsibility does not vanish during transfer
- receiving responsibility begins only after explicit admission
- escalation between domains does not erase prior trace

## Witness And Receipt Requirement

Every cross-domain transfer attempt must emit a receipt containing:

- originating domain
- receiving domain
- transfer candidate
- current standing basis
- transfer outcome
- responsible bearer or steward
- scope limits and exclusions
- timestamp or sequence position

Receipts must preserve whether the candidate was:

- admitted
- held
- refused
- or escalated

## Anti Contamination Invariant

The system must not allow:

- domain evidence to masquerade as domain standing
- one domain's refusal to silently contaminate all others
- one domain's admission to silently elevate all others
- cross-domain reuse without explicit scoping

The system must maintain clear distinction between:

- local standing
- transferred evidence
- new admission

## Participation Boundary

Only structures explicitly admitted within a domain may:

- act in that domain
- influence downstream state in that domain
- be treated as authoritative there

Transferred but unresolved candidates must not:

- participate as though already admitted
- inherit action permission from their origin domain
- blur responsibility between domains

## Completion Posture

This law completes the spatial integrity side of accountable agency.

The system is considered structurally ready at this seam when it can:

- preserve local legitimacy
- transfer evidence without transferring standing
- require new discernment before new action
- name who bears origin and receiving consequence across domain boundaries

## Minimal Form

> What stands here does not stand everywhere.
> What was admitted there is evidence here, not permission.
> Move across domains only by re-discernment.
> Let transfer carry trace, not borrowed legitimacy.
