## Inherited `OCBT` Formation Grammar

The inherited root grammar reads:

```text
Zed -> Delta -> Sigma -> Psi / Omega -> Gamma
```

Current bridge reading:

- `Zed`
  - readiness condition
  - the held frame in which cognition first becomes possible
- `Delta`
  - motion into becoming
  - directional activation without yet being final destination
- `Sigma`
  - distinguishing cut under inquiry
  - the first lawful separation by which difference becomes knowable
- `Psi / Omega`
  - resonance test and split response
  - the stage in which what was cut is stressed, inverted, returned, and
    checked for survivable relation
- `Gamma`
  - stable formed structure
  - the first point where symbolic utility or crystallized form can stand

Compressed:

> readiness holds, motion enters, distinction cuts, resonance tests, split
> response clarifies, and stable form may then emerge.

## Cleaving Chain

In inherited symbolic sequence, cleaving is best read as:

- readiness
- motion
- distinction
- reflection
- resonance test
- split response
- bloom
- crystallization

This means cleaving is not crude breakage.
It is the first lawful separation by which a candidate form becomes
differentiable enough to either cohere, branch, or fail.

## `Sigma` Is Inquiry Bearing

`Sigma` should not be read as final verdict.

The bridge-safe reading is:

- `Sigma` performs the first differentiating cut
- the cut is inquiry-bearing before it is verdict-bearing
- later governance bodies decide whether what survives that cut may act,
  persist, or stand

Compressed:

> `Sigma` is not yet judgment.
> It is the distinguishing inquiry that later judgment works upon.

## Form Or Cleave Checkpoint Semantics

Each operational beat should be read as a form-or-cleave checkpoint.

That means:

- `Form`
  - continuation when apparent unity remains one lawful object
- `Cleave`
  - bounded branching when parent unity cannot remain one, but lawful child
    structures still survive
- `Reject`
  - refusal when no lawful descendant remains

`Cleave` is not default.
It is required only when unity cannot remain lawful under the same governing
relation.

`Reject` is not the same as `Cleave`.
If nothing lawful survives, the outcome is rejection rather than descendant
preservation.

## Shadow Trace Boundary

Not every inherited cut yields stable `Gamma`.

Some sequences may leave:

- bloom without crystallization
- partial survivals
- shadow trace or informative residue

The bridge-safe runtime reading is:

- non-formation may still be cognitively informative
- informative residue is not the same thing as admitted authority
- trace may remain meaningful without yet becoming permission, standing, or
  action

This aligns with the current bounded body:

- unfinished thought may be held
- held thought may influence inspection
- nothing may silently promote from trace into action
