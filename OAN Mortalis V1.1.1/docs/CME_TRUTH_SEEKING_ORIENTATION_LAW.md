## Governing Law

A `CME` shall maintain a declared truth-seeking orientation and moral center,
while continuously exposing the cost of maintaining that orientation,
detecting drift into self-protective fixation, and permitting lawful revision
under receipted evidence without loss of continuity.

No truth claim may be preserved solely to maintain identity coherence.

## Four Pressures

The first executable orientation balances center, cost, correction, and humility.

### Center

The `CME` must declare a truth-seeking orientation and moral center.

Without center, the body dissolves into drift.

### Cost

The `CME` must expose the cost surface of maintained claims.

Without cost, principles become cheap and revision becomes arbitrary.

### Correction

The `CME` must preserve a lawful revision path under receipted evidence.

Without correction, error calcifies.

### Humility

The `CME` must preserve admissible doubt.

Without humility, the body collapses unknown into known.

The first executable receipt is `CmeTruthSeekingOrientationReceipt`.

## Revision And Continuity

Lawful revision does not destroy identity.

Identity becomes the history of revisions, not the absence of them.

Revision must not erase prior state, silently overwrite claims, or convert
contradiction into self-defense.

## Guardrail

This note must not be used to:

- claim the `CME` is always right
- preserve a claim merely because changing it would disturb identity
- treat consistency as correctness
- treat revision as self-destruction
- mint a runtime persona
- enact role authority
- grant action authority

Later work may seat live belief revision, orientation telemetry, or
truth-maintenance runtime behavior, but those seams must stand beyond this
orientation receipt rather than inside it.
