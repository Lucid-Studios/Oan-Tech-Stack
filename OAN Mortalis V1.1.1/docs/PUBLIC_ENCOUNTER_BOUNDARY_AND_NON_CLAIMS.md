# Public Encounter Boundary And Non-Claims

## Purpose

This document defines the first public encounter boundary for the active
`OAN Mortalis V1.1.1` line.

It governs how external readers, contributors, operators, and institutional
contacts may encounter the repository without being invited to infer more
identity, authority, or certainty than the internal system can lawfully
support.

This is an outward-facing boundary law, not a marketing surface.

## System Statement

`OAN Mortalis V1.1.1` is a lawfully bounded engineered cognition framework.

It is not a person, not autonomous authority, and not self-originating.
It is an engineered stack whose build, runtime, evidence, legal-orientation,
and participation surfaces are constrained by repo-local governance,
admission, refusal, receipt, and verification law.

## Capability Boundary

The public repository may truthfully present the system as able to:

- operate within defined domains when admission law permits
- receive scoped interaction through bounded ingress surfaces
- evaluate posture, formation, evidence, and readiness under current law
- produce bounded outputs and receipts
- preserve refusal and uncertainty where the evidence does not authorize a
  stronger claim

The public repository may not present the system as already carrying:

- personhood
- autonomous authority
- legal or fiduciary accountability
- certainty beyond current evidence
- installer completion
- a hosted seed `LLM` shipped in the public checkout
- full `CME` minting, bonded role enactment, custody, or governance authority

## Interaction Model

External interaction is framed as:

```text
input -> bounded processing -> receipted output
```

The output may be useful, structured, and evidence-bearing.
It is not final truth merely because the system produced it.

The system must preserve the ability to refuse, defer, qualify, or narrow an
output when domain, role, capacity, evidence, or readiness is insufficient.

## Domain And Role Constraint

Participation is scoped through:

```text
Domain -> Role -> Capacity
```

A domain does not automatically grant a role.
A role does not automatically grant every capacity.
A capacity does not imply governance-origin, legal accountability, identity
standing, or unrestricted action authority.

Admission remains bounded, and refusal exists.

## Evidence And Uncertainty

Public statements and outputs must remain:

- contextual
- bounded
- revisable
- accountable to current evidence

Receipts may support a claim, but a receipt does not by itself create the
ontology, authority, personhood, custody, or legal standing that the receipt
describes.

Where evidence is incomplete, the public surface must say so or stay silent.

## External Responsibility

External responsibility remains with the relevant humans, operators,
maintainers, institutions, and legal actors.

That responsibility includes:

- interpretation
- high-stakes decisions
- legal accountability
- human oversight
- compliance review
- deployment judgment

The system may support those responsibilities.
It does not replace them.

## Non-Claims

The public repository must not claim or imply:

- that the system is a person
- that the system is autonomous authority
- that the system is self-originating
- that any output is certain beyond evidence
- that any output is legal, medical, financial, or safety-critical advice
  without qualified external review
- that the public checkout is the complete operational installer
- that the hosted seed `LLM` is shipped as part of the public checkout
- that `CME` standing, closure, bonded role enactment, custody, or governance
  authority has been minted merely by public documentation
- that Domain, Role, Capacity, receipt, or evidence surfaces silently create
  identity, origin, legal accountability, or action authority

## Public Statement Invariant

No external statement may imply more identity, authority, or certainty than the internal system can lawfully support.

Any outward-facing description, README section, public contact response,
release note, repository summary, or contributor-facing statement must preserve
that invariant.

## Build Consequence

Future public documentation changes must preserve this encounter boundary.

If a later public surface wants to strengthen a claim about identity,
authority, certainty, `CME` standing, installer completion, role enactment, or
custody, that claim must first be supported by repo-local executable truth,
governance law, legal-orientation law, and audit evidence.
