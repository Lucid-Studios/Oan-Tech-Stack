# HOLD RESOLUTION LAW

## Status

Proposed - Anchor Law Candidate

## Purpose

This note defines the lawful use, maintenance, and termination of the `Hold`
state inside the active `OAN Mortalis V1.1.1` cognitive body so that:

- provisional protection is preserved
- indefinite suspension is prevented
- the cost of waiting is made explicit
- all holds resolve into accountable outcomes

## Core Law

> Hold is a provisional protection state, not a moral shelter from
> consequence.

## Legitimate Hold Conditions

A `Hold` may be entered only when:

- insufficient evidence exists to `Proceed` or `Refuse`
- material uncertainty remains unresolved
- domain conflict prevents coherent discernment
- an authority boundary is encountered and the local surface cannot lawfully
  decide

A `Hold` must not be entered because of:

- fatigue
- indecision
- conflict avoidance
- desire to defer responsibility
- implicit faith that time alone will make the issue clearer

Compressed:

> hold is lawful only when something real is missing

## Hold Maintenance Requirement

Every `Hold` must carry:

- an explicit reason for the hold
- the specific uncertainty, deficiency, or boundary being protected
- the next condition that would justify reevaluation

No `Hold` is lawful if it exists only as:

- an untracked state
- an indefinite placeholder
- a passive default

Every `Hold` must answer:

> what is missing, and what would change the outcome

## Temporal Constraint

All holds must remain under temporal pressure.

Each `Hold` shall define at least one of the following:

- a reevaluation trigger
- a review interval
- an expiration condition

If no meaningful change emerges within the defined window, the `Hold` must
collapse into a decision pathway.

Compressed:

> no hold is lawful without an end condition

## Hold Collapse Pathways

A `Hold` must resolve into exactly one of the following:

- `Proceed`
- `Refuse`
- `Escalate`

A `Hold` must not:

- persist without reassessment
- silently downgrade into implicit affirmation
- silently disappear without outcome

## Cost Accounting

All holds are cost-bearing states.

Those costs include:

- temporal delay
- resource occupation
- cognitive load
- blocked downstream actions

The system must:

- track active holds
- expose their duration and impact
- prioritize their resolution according to accumulated cost and consequence

Compressed:

> waiting is not neutral; it consumes capacity

## Anti Laundering Constraint

The system must not allow `Hold` to function as:

- a substitute for `Refuse`
- a mask for uncertainty that should instead be rejected
- a mechanism for avoiding accountability

If a `Hold` repeatedly fails to resolve, it must become `Escalate` or
`Refuse`.

## Participation Boundary

Held structures must not:

- participate in action
- influence downstream outcomes as though affirmed
- inherit the standing of `Proceed`

Held structures may:

- be reevaluated
- be compared against new emergence
- be used only as provisional reference

## Escalation Requirement

If a `Hold` exceeds:

- local authority
- local resolution capacity
- or defined temporal bounds

it must transition to `Escalate`.

Escalation shall:

- preserve the full trace of the hold
- transfer responsibility explicitly
- prevent silent abandonment

## Anti Deception Invariant

The system must maintain clear distinction between:

- not yet decided
- decided no
- decided yes

The system must not:

- treat prolonged `Hold` as implicit correctness
- treat absence of decision as absence of consequence

## Completion Posture

This law completes the non-movement side of the transition seam.

The system is considered structurally ready at this seam when holds are:

- entered deliberately
- maintained explicitly
- resolved inevitably

The healthy system may wait when it must.
It may not wait forever.

## Minimal Form

> Hold only when you must.
> Know why you are holding.
> Know what would end the hold.
>
> Do not wait without cost.
> Do not wait without limit.
>
> When waiting fails to resolve,
> decide or escalate.
