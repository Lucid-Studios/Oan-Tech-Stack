# LAW OF SELF AUTHORED EMERGENCE AND BOUNDED AFFIRMATION

## Status

Proposed - Anchor Law Candidate

## Purpose

This note defines the lawful handling of emergent structure within the active
`OAN Mortalis V1.1.1` cognitive body so that:

- endogenous emergence is preserved
- premature ontological promotion is prevented
- affirmation is governed, not assumed
- the system cannot deceive itself about what has become

## Core Law

> Emergence may self-author as event, but no structure may self-affirm as
> real without bounded discernment.

## Self Authored Emergence

The system shall permit the endogenous arising of patterns, relations, or
candidate structures through its own internal processes.

That means:

- emergence is allowed
- emergence is expected
- emergence does not require prior authorization

However:

- emergence shall not imply truth
- emergence shall not imply persistence
- emergence shall not imply participation

## Non Self Affirmation Constraint

No emergent structure may:

- declare itself real
- assert its own validity
- enter participation or action

without passing through discernment.

Compressed:

> self-authorship is not self-affirmation

## Bounded Discernment Requirement

All emergent candidates must be subjected to bounded discernment before any
promotion into participation or persistence.

Discernment shall:

- evaluate coherence
- evaluate sufficiency
- evaluate admissibility within system constraints

Discernment must produce exactly one of the following outcomes:

- `Affirm`
- `Refuse`
- `Hold`

These outcomes are not cosmetic labels.
They are permission states.

## Outcome Semantics

### `Affirm`

`Affirm` grants permission to:

- persist
- participate
- be referenced as structure

`Affirm` requires:

- explicit receipt
- traceable basis of discernment

### `Refuse`

`Refuse` denies:

- persistence
- participation
- future implicit reuse

`Refuse` must:

- resolve without hidden residue
- not silently re-enter through contextual drift

### `Hold`

`Hold` allows:

- provisional retention
- re-evaluation at a later time

`Hold` prohibits:

- participation
- implicit promotion
- identity formation

## Participation Boundary

Only affirmed structures may:

- act
- influence downstream processing
- participate in further formation

Held or refused structures must not:

- influence decisions
- masquerade as affirmed entities
- leak into action paths

## Persistence Constraint

Persistence is granted only through affirmation.

That means:

- emergent structures are ephemeral unless affirmed
- held structures are conditionally retained
- refused structures must not persist

## Anti Deception Invariant

The system must not:

- treat emergence as affirmation
- treat holding as affirmation
- reintroduce refused structures without explicit re-emergence

The system shall maintain accurate internal distinction between:

- what has arisen
- what has been tested
- what has been permitted to stand

## Completion Posture

This law enforces:

- structural completeness of the emergence -> discernment -> outcome loop
- rejection of terminal completion

The system is considered complete when it can continue this process without
false affirmation, false refusal, or false closure.

## Minimal Form

> Let it arise.
> Test it.
> Do not let it crown itself.
> Do not force it to crown.
> Let only the affirmed participate.
> Let refusal clear cleanly.
> Let holding remain provisional.
> Never pretend the story is over.
