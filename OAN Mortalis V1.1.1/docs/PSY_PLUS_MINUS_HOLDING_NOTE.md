# PSY PLUS MINUS HOLDING NOTE

## Status

Proposed - Structural Note Candidate

## Purpose

This note defines the lawful cryptic chamber for pre-admissible constructs
inside the active `OAN Mortalis V1.1.1` cognitive body.

It exists so the system may hold unfinished thought without:

- prematurely collapsing it into action
- prematurely discarding it as noise
- silently treating it as permission

## Core Statement

> `Psy ±` is the structured retention of not-yet-admissible constructs within
> the cryptic working surface, allowing iterative evaluation without premature
> commitment.

This note is structural, not juridical.
It defines a lawful chamber for unfinished thought.
It does not grant permission, action, or standing by itself.

## Chamber Scope

`Psy ±` holds constructs that are:

- not yet affirmed
- not yet refused
- not yet admissible for action

This chamber is for pre-admissible cognition only.

It is not:

- a replacement for `CCM`
- a replacement for `TAB`
- a hidden permission surface

## `Psy+`

`Psy+` holds constructs that:

- appear potentially admissible
- align with current structure
- may become eligible for later affirmation

`Psy+` does not itself affirm.

## `Psy-`

`Psy-` holds constructs that:

- remain uncertain
- conflict with known structure
- or cannot yet be resolved

`Psy-` does not itself refuse.

It preserves caution without forcing immediate exclusion.

## Influence Boundary

`Psy ±` may inform interpretation at the cryptic edge.

That means:

- it may shape what `Compass` inspects
- it may shape what is considered for discernment

It may not:

- grant permission
- trigger `Proceed`
- alter role
- alter domain
- alter thresholds

Compressed:

> `Psy ±` may influence attention.
> It may not authorize action.

## Isolation Requirement

The chamber must remain isolated from action authority.

No `Psy ±` construct is lawful if it silently:

- enters `CCM` as authority
- enters `TAB` as permission
- changes standing by continued presence alone

## Reversibility Requirement

Entries in `Psy ±` must be discardable without side effects.

That means:

- dropping a held construct does not alter standing
- dropping a held construct does not alter permission
- dropping a held construct does not create hidden residue

## Exit Paths

Any `Psy ±` construct may exit only by:

- `Affirmation`
- `Refusal`
- continued cryptic `Hold`

No exit path may skip discernment and revalidation where those are required.

## Guardrails

The system must prevent:

- implicit carry into `CCM` as authority
- silent promotion into action
- confidence creep without revalidation

## Non Overlap

`Psy ±` is not `CCM`.

- `Psy ±` holds unfinished thought
- `CCM` carries admitted continuity

`Psy ±` is not `REVALIDATION`.

- `Psy ±` holds the not-yet-admissible
- `REVALIDATION` judges whether the already-carried remains currently valid

`Psy ±` is not `TAB`.

- `Psy ±` shapes unfinished cognition
- `TAB` governs lawful action and accountability

## Completion Posture

The chamber is structurally healthy when:

- unfinished thought may be held without premature commitment
- uncertainty may remain active without leaking into permission
- constructs may be dropped cleanly when they do not survive discernment

## Minimal Form

> Hold what is not yet ready.
> Let it inform inspection.
> Do not let it authorize action.
> Let it be dropped without residue.
