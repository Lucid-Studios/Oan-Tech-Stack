# PRIVATE DOMAIN SERVICE WITNESS LAW

## Purpose

This note seats the first private-domain service witness seam above domain and
role admission.

It exists because accepted operational jurisdiction is not enough by itself to
prove accountable local action.

After `DomainAdmissionRecord`, the service layer must be able to say:

> who did what with what and how within where and who

That grammar is not action execution.

It is a locally accountable witness surface.

## Governing Law

The private-domain service layer shall attest relation, not action execution.

It may preserve a structured accountability grammar for locally accountable
service events only when an accepted domain admission exists and the required
provenance axes are visible.

It may not execute action, enact the cradle-local governing surface, apply
`Mother/Father` origin authority, or convert service witness into semantic
promotion.

## Regional, Local, And Private Split

Regional service posture carries broad readiness, shared law, common policy,
and general admissibility.

Local service witness carries site-bound authority, instrumented action, and
bounded witness posture.

Private-domain witness carries identity-bearing continuity, accountable
intervention, and receipted jurisdiction over a retained standing body.

These layers are not interchangeable.

A regional posture may not pretend to be private relation.

A local service witness may not pretend the retained standing body and
continuity burden are already fully attested.

## Witness Receipt

The first executable receipt is `PrivateDomainServiceWitnessReceipt`.

It preserves:

- accepted domain admission basis
- actor handle
- action handle
- instrument handle
- method handle
- locality handle
- standing context handle
- continuity burden handles
- result receipt handles
- whether each required accountability axis is present
- whether private-domain relation is attested
- explicit action-execution withholding
- explicit cradle-local governance enactment withholding
- custodial-memory-only status
- candidate-only status

The receipt is custodial memory with lawful operational trace.

It does not perform the operation it witnesses.

## Required Axes

The first private-domain witness must preserve these axes:

- who initiated
- what changed or was acted upon
- with what instrument
- how it was performed
- where jurisdictionally it occurred
- under whose retained standing or continuity burden it now resides

If these axes are incomplete, the service layer may still expose regional or
local posture, but private-domain relation remains unattested.

## Refusal And Deferral

The witness must defer when:

- domain admission has not been accepted
- local action axes are incomplete
- private standing context is missing
- continuity burden is missing

The witness must refuse when:

- action execution is requested through this seam
- cradle-local governing surface enactment is requested through this seam

## Guardrail

This note must not be used to:

- run live `proc` execution
- select a cradle-local governing surface
- apply `Mother/Father` roles
- claim origin authority
- overwrite first Prime standing
- treat service witness as semantic rise

Later work may seat live action execution or cradle-local governance enactment,
but those seams must stand beyond this witness law rather than inside it.

Accepted private-domain witness may feed
`CME_MINIMUM_LEGAL_FOUNDING_BUNDLE_LAW.md` as operational provenance custody.

That handoff remains founding evidence only.
