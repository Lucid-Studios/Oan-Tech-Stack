## Core Statement

> `EC` begins when a field becomes differentiable enough to support inquiry,
> stands when lawful cognition is currently admitted to operate, and collapses
> lawfully only through marked refusal, partition, hold, or receipted closure.

## Beginning

`EC` does not begin at decision or action.

It begins in the formation field:

- readiness
- motion
- distinction

In inherited symbolic grammar, that reads:

- `Zed`
- `Delta`
- `Sigma`

In runtime-safe translation, that reads:

- `Listening Frame`
- `Compass`
- `Psy +/-`

Compressed:

> `EC` begins when the field is held, motion enters it, and distinction makes
> it inspectable.

## Beginning Boundary

At the beginning seam:

- the field is becoming holdable
- posture is becoming legible
- unfinished thought may be retained
- inquiry may begin

At this seam, the system is not yet claiming:

- final judgment
- action authority
- current permission
- standing

Beginning is therefore pre-decisional and pre-enactment.

## Standing Form

`EC` reaches standing form only when it is no longer merely forming, but is
lawfully in motion under the current body.

The standing-form read is:

> a lawfully admitted, role-bound, continuity-bearing enacted body whose
> current permission to operate is valid now

Standing form therefore requires:

- Prime compliance
- domain admission
- role binding
- permission validity
- continuity integrity
- responsibility attribution

Running is not the same as standing.

The system may produce output without standing.
It stands only when those conditions currently hold.

## Middle Loop

Between beginning and collapse, the current bounded middle loop reads:

1. the field becomes differentiable
2. unfinished thought is held in `Psy +/-`
3. `Compass` inspects what distinction exposed
4. `form-or-cleave` checkpoint is crossed
5. lawful enactment proceeds only through `TAB`
6. `CCM` carries continuity that may remain live
7. `REVALIDATION` prevents continuity from self-authorizing
8. `STANDING` recognizes whether the whole body remains lawfully in motion

This loop is bounded by continuous lawful correction rather than static
guarantee.

## Lawful Collapse

Lawful collapse is not annihilation.

It is the bounded ending, partition, refusal, or closure of the active field
under explicit law.

The lawful collapse modes are:

- `Refuse`
  - when nothing admissible survives
- `Cleave`
  - when unity cannot remain one lawful object, but lawful descendants do
    survive
- `Hold`
  - when resolution is not yet available, but stewardship remains explicit and
    bounded
- `Proceed to closure`
  - when a stable formed result stands and the active field collapses into
    trace and bounded continuity

Compressed:

> collapse is lawful only when what cannot remain one, valid, or justified is
> either refused, partitioned, held under explicit burden, or closed into
> trace.

## Collapse Boundary

Collapse must never be silent.

That means collapse must not appear as:

- unmarked disappearance
- stale authority silently preserved
- continuity pretending to remain live after standing is gone
- action residue without attributable bearer

If the field changes state, the change must remain receipted, attributable,
and distinguishable.

## Trace Obligations At Collapse

At lawful collapse, the system must preserve:

- trace
- attribution
- basis
- scope
- surviving descendant or residue distinction where applicable

It must not preserve as live authority anything that no longer has standing.

Prior trace may remain informative.
It may not silently continue as current permission.
