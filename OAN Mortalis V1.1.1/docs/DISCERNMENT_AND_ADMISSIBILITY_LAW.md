# DISCERNMENT_AND_ADMISSIBILITY_LAW

## Purpose

This law defines the conditions under which a provisional predicate, frame, or
specification may be promoted into closure-bearing standing within
`OAN Mortalis V1.1.1`.

The line must distinguish between:

- transport of meaning
- formation of meaning
- admission of meaning
- implementation from admitted standing

No predicate may be promoted merely because it is present, fluent, repeated, or
successfully transported.

## Governing Rule

A lawful build must know the difference between transport, formation,
admission, and implementation.

That means:

- transport is not formation
- formation is not admission
- admission is not implementation
- rhetorical fluency is not admissibility
- repetition is not promotion warrant

## Core Doctrine

- `Dialectic` builds the predicate field.
- `Rhetoric` carries partially formed meaning between agents and surfaces.
- `Discernment` tests candidate structures against defined conditions.
- `Yes/No` records lawful closure under those conditions.

A lawful build must know the difference between:

- what is being explored
- what is being communicated
- what is being judged
- what is being admitted

## Standing Classes

### Rhetoric-Bearing Surfaces

These may carry partially formed, exploratory, or negotiative meaning.
They do not, by themselves, establish closure.

Examples:

- `provisional`
- `clarify`
- `frame-now`
- `spec-now`

These surfaces may contain ambiguity, approximation, and active construction,
provided they do not falsely claim closure.

### Closure-Bearing Surfaces

These establish committed standing under defined conditions.

Examples:

- `admit`
- `implement-now`
- `ready`

No item may enter these surfaces without satisfying admissibility conditions.

### Refusal And Non-Admission Surfaces

These preserve system integrity when admissibility is not met or contradiction
is present.

Examples:

- `hold`
- `refuse`
- `fault-recoverable`
- `withheld_or_escalated`

These do not indicate failure of motion.
They indicate lawful non-closure.

## Admissibility Conditions

A predicate candidate is admissible only when all required conditions are met:

1. `defined-terms`
   The relevant terms and boundaries are sufficiently specified for evaluation.
2. `contextual-scope`
   The candidate identifies the conditions under which it is being evaluated.
3. `evidence-sufficiency`
   The supporting receipts, observations, or witness structures are sufficient
   for the standing being requested.
4. `non-contradiction`
   No unresolved contradiction exists within the active field of evaluation.
5. `surface-appropriateness`
   The candidate is being advanced on a surface authorized to bear the
   requested closure.
6. `promotion-warrant`
   The requested standing is proportionate to the available evidence and does
   not exceed it.

If any of these fail, the candidate must remain provisional, be held for
clarification, or be refused.

## Provisional Retention Conditions

A candidate remains provisional when:

- terms are still under construction
- context is incomplete
- evidence is suggestive but not sufficient
- multiple admissible interpretations remain open
- contradiction conditions have not yet been resolved
- the candidate is still rhetorical transport rather than closure-bearing
  standing

Provisional standing is not error.
It is lawful non-closure during active formation.

## Refusal Conditions

A candidate must be refused, withheld, or escalated when:

- contradiction is present under shared conditions
- required receipts are absent
- the claimed standing exceeds evidence
- closure is being asserted on a rhetoric-only surface
- the requested promotion would corrupt continuity or downstream trust
- implementation is being implied before admission is complete

## Promotion Rule

No provisional structure earns promotion by repetition, fluency, urgency, or convenience.

Promotion occurs only when discernment determines that:

- the predicate field is sufficiently formed
- the evidence is sufficient
- contradiction has been resolved or explicitly bounded
- the target surface is authorized to bear closure
- the requested standing does not outrun the current layer of the stack

## Minimum Receipts For Promotion

Before a predicate may move from rhetoric-bearing standing into
closure-bearing standing, the promotion surface must carry at minimum:

- `candidateId`
- `requestedStandingClass`
- `targetSurfaceClass`
- `definedTermsState`
- `contextScopeClass`
- `evidenceSufficiencyState`
- `contradictionState`
- `promotionWarrantState`
- `witnessStatus`
- `authorizationBasis`
- `continuityImpactClass`
- `discernmentDisposition`
- `promotionDecision`

The minimum admissible values are:

- `definedTermsState = defined-enough`
- `contextScopeClass = explicit`
- `evidenceSufficiencyState = sufficient-for-requested-standing`
- `contradictionState = none-unresolved` or `bounded-and-non-corrupting`
- `promotionWarrantState = proportionate`
- `witnessStatus = witnessed`
- `discernmentDisposition = admissible`
- `promotionDecision = admit` or stronger authorized closure

If these receipt conditions are not met, the candidate must remain on a
provisional surface or move into refusal handling.

## System Warning

The primary corruption risk is not inability to produce.
It is inability to refuse correctly.

A system that transports well without lawful discernment may stabilize false
standing, producing continuity of error rather than continuity of truth.

## Compression

> Dialectic builds the predicate field.  
> Discernment tests candidates within it.  
> Rhetoric carries them between minds.  
> Yes/No seals the result.

> A lawful build must know the difference between transport, formation,
> admission, and implementation.
