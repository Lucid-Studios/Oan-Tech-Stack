# SANCTUARY BIAD AND CRADLETEK GOVERNING SURFACE NOTE

## Purpose

This note corrects a recurring conflation in the active `V1.1.1` line.

It keeps four things distinct:

- the singular Sanctuary biad
- the Steward as the third witnessing and braid-issuing line
- the repeatable admitted `CradleTek` body
- the later single governing surface carried by a specific cradle instance

The goal is to keep resonance, heartbeat, and later standing-wave work from
being framed on the wrong body map.

## Core Correction

`Sanctuary` is governed by the singular constitutional biad:

- `Mother` for Prime-side governance
- `Father` for Cryptic-side governance

That biad belongs to Sanctuary itself.

It is not re-seated for every admitted `CradleTek` instance.

`CradleTek` is the repeatable extended body beneath Sanctuary.

It may later receive one governing `CME` surface for its extended set, but it
does not re-enact the Sanctuary biad as though each cradle were a new
constitutional world.

`Steward` is the third line.

Its work is narrower:

- witness the standing relation
- issue or witness a unique cryptic braid for the specific cradle instance
- preserve the lawful handoff from Sanctuary-origin governance into bounded
  cradle-local governance

In compact form:

> Sanctuary holds the biad. Steward braids the cradle. CradleTek receives one
> governing surface beneath that braid.
