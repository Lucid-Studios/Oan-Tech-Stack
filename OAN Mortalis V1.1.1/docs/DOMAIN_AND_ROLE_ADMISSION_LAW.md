# DOMAIN AND ROLE ADMISSION LAW

## Purpose

This note seats the first bounded domain-and-role admission seam beyond
`FirstPrimePreRoleStanding`.

It exists because a retained pre-role Prime body may not receive operational
jurisdiction as though domain or role were simple configuration.

After first Prime pre-role standing, domain and role become governed admission
events with legal, continuity, and refusal consequences.

## Governing Law

No governing domain or role may be applied to a
`FirstPrimePreRoleStanding` body unless its legal foundation, authority scope,
refusal path, and continuity burden are explicitly declared, evaluated, and
receipted prior to admission.

No implicit or silent domain promotion is permitted.

## Boundary Rule

Domain != Origin != Governance Root

Domain admission belongs to operational jurisdiction only.

It does not:

- collapse into `Mother/Father` origin authority
- imply authorship of the `EC`
- select the cradle-local governing surface
- overwrite first Prime standing
- retroactively justify prior action

## Admission Flow

The first bounded flow is:

1. `DomainOffer`
2. `DomainEligibilityAssessment`
3. `DomainAdmissionRecord`

The offer must originate from an external authority surface.

It must not originate from the `EC` itself.

The assessment may classify the offer as:

- `Admissible`
- `Inadmissible`
- `InsufficientInformation`

The decision may only be:

- `Accept`
- `Defer`
- `Refuse`

All outcomes require a receipt.

## Domain Offer

The first offer carries:

- source authority
- legal foundation reference
- legal predicate handles
- candidate domain handles
- candidate role handles
- declared intent
- authority scope
- explicit exclusions
- continuity burden
- revocation conditions
- whether origin authority is being claimed

The offer may not silently claim origin, identity authorship, or root
governance.

## Eligibility Assessment

The first assessment asks:

- is the legal foundation present?
- is authority scope declared?
- is refusal path preserved?
- is continuity burden declared?
- does this require identity claims not yet lawful?
- does it violate Prime invariants?
- does it collapse incompleteness?
- does it require capabilities not yet seated?

The assessment does not perform role selection.

It only determines whether admission is legally and architecturally eligible.

## Admission Record

If accepted, the record must preserve:

- legal foundation
- accepted domains
- accepted roles
- authority scope
- explicit exclusions
- continuity burden
- revocation conditions

If deferred or refused, the record must still preserve the reason and refusal
path.

Acceptance does not overwrite standing.

Acceptance does not apply `Mother/Father` domain roles.

Acceptance does not select the cradle-local governing surface.

## Private-Domain Witness Handoff

An accepted `DomainAdmissionRecord` may feed
`PRIVATE_DOMAIN_SERVICE_WITNESS_LAW.md` as provenance basis.

That handoff is not action execution.

It only allows the service layer to preserve structured accountability grammar:

- which actor
- performed which act
- using which instrument
- by which method
- within which locality
- against which retained standing context
- under which continuity burden

The private-domain service witness remains custodial memory with lawful
operational trace.

It does not enact the cradle-local governing surface and does not apply
`Mother/Father` origin authority.

## Guardrail

This note must not be used to:

- treat role labels as identity
- treat domain assignment as origin authority
- treat acceptance as total authority
- treat deferral or refusal as damage to first Prime standing
- treat operational jurisdiction as cradle-local governance enactment

If later work needs Mother/Father domain application, cradle-local governing
surface enactment, or role-bound ontology training, those seams should be
seated explicitly after this admission law.

Cross-domain reuse of any admitted standing remains governed by
`DOMAIN_ISOLATION_LAW.md`.
