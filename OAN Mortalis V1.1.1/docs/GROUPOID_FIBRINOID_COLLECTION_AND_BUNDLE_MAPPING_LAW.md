# GROUPOID_FIBRINOID_COLLECTION_AND_BUNDLE_MAPPING_LAW

## Purpose

This note begins the bounded lattice work for groupoid data collection in the
active `OAN Mortalis V1.1.1` line.

It exists because the line already has:

- the primary domain/spline categorical read in
  `V1_1_1_DOMAIN_AND_SPLINE_GROUPOID_AUDIT.md`
- the semantic-class versus package-class correction in
  `TELEMETRY_BUNDLE_AND_GROUPOID_TAXONOMY.md`

What the line did not yet have was a lawful candidate-only collection body
that can gather that tuple into one bounded artifact and map it into bundle
packaging without letting packaging redefine meaning.

## Governing Compression

The governing correction is:

> a fibrinoid is a candidate-only lattice collection body read through the
> shared groupoid tuple.

The second correction is:

> bundle mapping preserves packaging only; it does not promote admission,
> inheritance, or semantic redefinition.

This seam begins the lattice work.
It does not yet authorize higher-order lattice condensation, inherited
formation, or semantic promotion by packaging habit.

## First Fibrinoid Tuple

The first fibrinoid collection body should be readable through:

- `domain`
- `spline`
- `semanticClass`
- `authorityClass`
- `continuityClass`
- `retentionClass`

A first fibrinoid may additionally carry:

- pointer handles
- symbolic carrier handles
- lattice notes

These adjuncts help later discernment and bundle mapping, but they do not
replace the tuple.

## Bundle Mapping Rule

A fibrinoid may be mapped into one first-pass packaging class:

- `state_surface`
- `run_bundle`
- `daily_bundle`
- `report_readout`

The first mapping rules are:

- `standing_surface` candidates may remain `state_surface` when they are still
  projecting current posture rather than event proof
- `daily_compacted` retention maps to `daily_bundle`
- report-facing derived readouts may map to `report_readout`
- all remaining first-pass candidates default to `run_bundle`

In every case:

- package class does not redefine semantic class
- candidate-only fibrinoids do not become admitted or inherited by mapping
  alone
- preserved distinction must remain visible or the fibrinoid is not ready for
  later lattice condensation

## Interpretation Consequences

The practical reading rules are:

- a fibrinoid is not yet a receipt, ledger, witness, or inherited lattice body
- a bundle pointer naming a fibrinoid package does not tell the full semantic
  meaning of what it carries
- mapping into bundle packaging is lawful collection, not promotion
- later lattice condensation, reduction, and inheritance require separate law
