# PRIME MEMBRANE PROJECTED BRAID HISTORY INTERPRETATION LAW

## Purpose

This note defines the first bounded membrane-side interpretation law for
projected braid history in the active `V1.1.1` line.

It exists to keep five things distinct:

- active projection
- stable braid
- return candidate
- defer-only evidence
- per-line residue that must remain visible even inside a coherent braid

This note does not grant membrane receipt or Prime closure.
It only seats the first lawful read by which the membrane may classify
projected plurality without flattening preserved distinction.
Membrane interpretation may not treat classification as receipt or closure.

## Core Read

The active stack should now read projected braid history this way:

- projected plurality may arrive at the membrane as history without becoming
  receipt
- coherent braid is readable, but it is not the same thing as closure
- preserved line distinction must remain visible even when the braid is stable
- advisory return signals may be classified without being treated as Prime-side
  retention
- unstable or flattened history must remain defer-only evidence

In compact form:

> membrane interpretation may classify projected braid history, but it may not
> erase preserved distinction, and it may not treat classification as receipt
> or closure.

## First Interpretation Bands

The first bounded interpretation family is:

- `ActiveProjection`
- `StableBraid`
- `ReturnCandidate`
- `DeferOnlyEvidence`

These are not closure classes.
They are the smallest lawful read-set by which the membrane can stop treating
projected plurality as one flat stream.

## Visible Residue Rule

Projected braid history must keep these residues visible:

- line handle
- source surface handle
- residual projection posture
- clustered versus swarmed participation
- accepted contribution handles
- whether distinction remained preserved

If that residue cannot remain visible, the membrane must not pretend to have
received a stable coherent braid.

## First Invariant

The first membrane-side invariant is:

> membrane interpretation may classify projected braid history, but it must
> preserve per-line distinction, may not erase preserved distinction, and may
> not treat classification as receipt or closure.

That means:

- interpretation is not receipt
- receipt is not closure
- advisory return is not Prime retention
- coherent braid is not license to flatten the contributing lines

## Immediate Build Consequence

Any future membrane-side read in this area should say plainly:

- whether the history is only active projection
- whether the history has reached stable braid
- whether the history is only a return candidate
- whether the history must remain defer-only evidence
- which per-line residues remain visible in the membrane read
- how any later membrane-side receipt still preserves visible distinction and
  keeps closure withheld

If it cannot say that plainly, the membrane should keep the history classified
as active projection or defer-only evidence instead of pretending it already
has a flattened whole.
