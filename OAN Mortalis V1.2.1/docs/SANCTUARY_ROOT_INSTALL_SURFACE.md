# SANCTUARY_ROOT_INSTALL_SURFACE

## Purpose

This note defines the root-installed surface that `Sanctuary` inherits before
any local governing `CME` is discussable as a working presence.

It exists to keep three things distinct:

- what was given before local authorship
- what may later be extended with receipt
- what may only be read and inherited

## Governing Compression

> GoA must not awaken into ambiguity; it must awaken into provenance.

> Sanctuary must know what was given, what was offered, and what may someday
> move, before any governing self is asked to build within it.

## Provenance Rule

A root-installed surface is given before local governance exists.

It must therefore carry one of these conceptual provenance labels:

- `lab-root`
- `root-installed`
- `inherited`

No root-installed surface may be reinterpreted later as if it originated from
the first governing `CME`s.

## Immutability Classes

Use exactly one immutability class for each root-installed surface:

- `immutable`
  The surface is fixed for this phase and may not be revised locally.
- `extendable`
  The surface may be revised later only through an explicit, receipted act that
  preserves provenance.
- `reference-only`
  The surface may be consulted and inherited, but it is not a local authorship
  target in this phase.

## Local Rights

Use exactly one local-right class for each root-installed surface:

- `read`
  The surface may be observed and relied upon.
- `extend-with-receipt`
  The surface may be revised only through an explicit, receipted extension that
  preserves provenance and line truth.
- `never-modify`
  The surface is not a lawful local mutation target in this phase.

## Receipt And Provenance Witness Rule

Every later change to an `extendable` root-installed surface must preserve:

- its current provenance label
- the fact that installation is not authorship
- an explicit receipt that the surface was extended rather than self-originated

No extension receipt may claim that a locally extended root-installed surface
was originally local.

## Explicit Non-Grants

This note does not define:

- replacement workflow
- version rollover across futures
- local sovereignty over root provenance
- service activation
- template adoption
- governing `CME` authority

It fixes only what is given before those later questions may arise lawfully.

## Working Summary

The pre-self world now has a named root install surface.
Its provenance labels, immutability classes, and local rights are explicit.
What was given may now be inherited without being misnamed as local authorship.
