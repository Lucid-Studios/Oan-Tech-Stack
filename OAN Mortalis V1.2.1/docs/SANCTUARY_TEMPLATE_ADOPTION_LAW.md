# SANCTUARY_TEMPLATE_ADOPTION_LAW

## Purpose

This note defines what counts as a template in `V1.2.1` and how a template may
be adopted lawfully without turning availability into binding.

It exists to keep three things distinct:

- template existence
- explicit local adoption
- later active consequence

## What Counts As A Template

A template is a lawful starter form offered for local uptake.

In this phase, a template may be:

- a service-form office such as `Sanctuary.RTME`
- a starter manifest or bounded shape offered for later local uptake
- a seeded law form that may be instantiated later without being active now

A template is not merely any document or any nearby note.

## Adoption Act

No template becomes locally binding by mere presence.

Adoption must be:

- explicit
- receipted
- attributable to a named adopting office
- clear about which template is being adopted

Silent adoption is unlawful.

Downloaded or templated agreements remain templates until explicit adoption and
explicit assent.

No `AgreementPredicateBundle` may be formed from template presence alone.

## Adoption Classes

Use exactly one adoption class when a template is offered:

- `mandatory`
  The template must eventually be adopted before a later gate may lift.
- `optional`
  The template may be refused without invalidating the current phase.
- `conditional`
  The template becomes required only if a later lawful condition is met.

## Post-Adoption Invariants

After a lawful adoption act occurs, the following must remain explicit:

- the identity of the originating template
- the provenance of the offered form
- which fields remain fixed by the template
- which fields remain locally editable after adoption
- the receipt that proved the adoption act

No adoption may erase template origin.
No adoption may widen authority beyond what the adopting act named.

## Lawful Non-Adoption

Refusal to instantiate a template remains lawful unless the template is later
admitted under `mandatory` conditions.

Lawful non-adoption is not dishonesty, failure, or silent default.
It is a named protected outcome.

## Explicit Non-Grants

This note does not define:

- adoption automation
- template inheritance chains
- cascading local uptake
- service activation
- runtime consequence

Templating is not adoption.
Adoption is not activation.

## Working Summary

Templates may now be named as offered forms without becoming binding by
existence alone.
Adoption must be explicit and receipted.
Lawful non-adoption remains preserved.
