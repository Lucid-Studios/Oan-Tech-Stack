# SANCTUARYID GOA PREDICATE TRUTH BOUNDARY LAW

## Purpose

This note defines the lower predicate floor that `SanctuaryID.GoA` may
truthfully stand on in `V1.2.1`.

It exists to stop three forms of drift:

- claiming the lower floor is blank when it is not
- claiming the lower floor is mature when it is not
- treating withheld predicate authority as if it were already available

## Governing Compression

> `SanctuaryID.GoA` may govern only on top of predicate truth that already
> stands; it may not borrow predicate maturity from a later CradleTek or CME
> future.
> ingress-engrammitization is real; engram-predicate promotion is still
> withheld.

## Already-Real Predicate Floor

The already-real predicate floor in this phase is:

- `RootAtlas` remains remote source ancestry
- `Sanctuary.GEL` is the first lawful local substrate
- ingress-engrammitization is already real
- predicate landing is already real

These surfaces may be recognized, witnessed, and receipted by governance.

They may not be treated as empty.
They may also not be overstated beyond their current lawful standing.

## Withheld Predicate Floor

The following remain intentionally withheld:

- engram-predicate promotion
- engram minting
- any claim that minted or promoted predicate authority already exists for
  governance

Predicate landing is therefore not the same as promoted predicate authority.

## Required Refusal Posture

Governance must refuse any request that outruns the actual `SLI` floor.

That includes any request that assumes:

- promoted predicate authority already exists
- minted engram authority already exists
- runtime maturity may be borrowed from a later CradleTek or `CME` future

## Explicit Non-Grants

This note does not grant:

- predicate promotion
- engram minting
- active governing `CME` standing
- service authorization
- CradleTek-dependent life

## Working Summary

`SanctuaryID.GoA` now has a truthful lower predicate boundary.
It may stand on remote Atlas provenance, local `Sanctuary.GEL`, ingress
engrammitization, and predicate landing, while refusing any authority claim
that outruns those already-real surfaces.
