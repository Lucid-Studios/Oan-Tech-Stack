# SOULFRAME_CGOA_CRYPTIC_SEAL_LAW

## Purpose

This note defines how `SoulFrame` establishes the bridge required for a fresh
`cGoA` cryptic seal in `V1.2.1`.

It exists to keep five things distinct:

- governance origin
- cradle-local `CME` spawn authority
- fresh `cGoA` braid formation
- `SoulFrame` bridge work
- cryptic seal and later `CME` formation

## Governing Compression

> `SoulFrame` does not originate governance and does not spawn `CME`s.
> Its office here is narrower: it builds the lawful bridge into a fresh `cGoA`
> so the cryptic seal may be established.
> That bridge is reached through the Steward Nexus control manifold under the
> `SanctuaryID.GoA -> CradleTekID.GoA` chain.

## Preconditions

Before `SoulFrame` may establish the bridge:

- `Sanctuary.GEL` must exist
- `SanctuaryID.RTME` must be lawfully issued
- `SanctuaryID.GoA` must have authorized downward connection
- `CradleTekID.GoA` must exist as cradle-local control
- a fresh `cGoA` lane must be instantiated for the specific future `CME`

## Bridge Rule

`SoulFrame` may then:

- build the `cGoA` bridge for that one line
- comingle lawful braid-state needed for the seal
- prepare the membrane conditions under which cryptic office may form

It may not:

- reuse a shared `cGoA` across multiple `CME`s
- bypass `CradleTekID.GoA`
- originate governance itself

## Seal Rule

The cryptic seal is not just a naming step.

It is the lawful transition by which:

- a fresh `cGoA` braid lane
- a hosted issued `RTME` service path
- and a bounded `SoulFrame` bridge

become a real cryptic working seat for later `CME` formation.

## Non-Collapse Rules

- `SoulFrame` is not the governance origin
- `SoulFrame` is not the cradle-local spawn authority
- `SoulFrame` does not replace `cGoA`
- no seal exists without a fresh one-`CME` `cGoA`
- no `CME` should be described as lawfully formed before the bridge and seal
  exist

## Implementation Consequences

`V1.2.1` should use this note when shaping:

- bridge receipts
- seal receipts
- first `cGoA` instantiation records
- later `CME` spawn implementation
- hosted `RTME` binding points during `SoulFrame` build-out

This note fixes the membrane and braid work that sits between service issuance
and later `CME` formation.
