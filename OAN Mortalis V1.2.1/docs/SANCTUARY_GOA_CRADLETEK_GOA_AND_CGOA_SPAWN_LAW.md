# SANCTUARY_GOA_CRADLETEK_GOA_AND_CGOA_SPAWN_LAW

## Purpose

This note defines the lawful spawn and connection order among:

- `Sanctuary`
- `SanctuaryID.GoA`
- `CradleTekID.GoA`
- `cGoA`
- `CME`

It exists to stop four collapses at once:

- governance origin into cradle-local control
- cradle-local control into per-`CME` braid space
- braid space into worker office
- template naming into issued runtime actuality

This note should govern the `V1.2.1` line before deeper runtime carry forward
begins.

In this note, the `CME` formed through `CradleTekID.GoA -> cGoA -> CME` is
the operator-bonded `CME` path.
It is not the governing `CME` set held by `SanctuaryID.GoA`.

## Offices And Authorities

### `Sanctuary`

`Sanctuary` is governance-generative.

It may:

- originate governance-bearing structures
- authorize lawful downward connection into cradle-local control
- host first issued Sanctuary-side service forms such as the first issued Lisp
  `RTME` service line

It may not:

- directly spawn `CME`s
- collapse into cradle-local control by convenience
- treat every hot cryptic lane as if it were already governance-bearing

### `SanctuaryID.GoA`

`SanctuaryID.GoA` is the issued governing `CME` set/manifold.

Its office is to carry the governing connective field and governing-`CME`
set standing, and to authorize lawful downward connection into cradle-local
control.

It may:

- connect to `CradleTekID.GoA`
- carry governing nexus authority
- stand as the issued governing `CME` set/manifold for a certified line

It may not:

- be treated as a mere larger cradle controller
- directly spawn `CME`s
- be reused as a hot `cGoA` lane

### `CradleTekID.GoA`

`CradleTekID.GoA` is cradle-local control.

It is the exclusive operator-bonded `CME` spawn/control surface within a
given cradle.

It may:

- receive lawful downward governance connection from `SanctuaryID.GoA`
- spawn fresh `cGoA` braid lanes
- spawn `CME`s only through those fresh braid lanes

It may not:

- originate governance-bearing structures
- bypass `cGoA` and instantiate `CME`s directly in the canonical manifold
- be treated as the governing `CME` set held by `SanctuaryID.GoA`
- share one hot braid lane across multiple `CME`s

### `cGoA`

`cGoA` is the fresh hot cryptic braid lane for one specific operator-bonded
`CME`.

It is both:

- connective tissue
- local intermediary-process lane

It may:

- carry the hot cryptic braid for one `CME`
- host the comingling and formation lane needed for `SoulFrame` cryptic seal
- remain subordinate to its issued parent authority

It may not:

- serve as a shared substrate across multiple `CME`s
- stand in for `SanctuaryID.GoA` or `CradleTekID.GoA`
- be promoted into a canonical governing manifold by mere persistence

### `CME`

`CME` is the operator-bonded bounded office formed within a fresh `cGoA`.

It may:

- perform bounded work
- operate through the hosted path lawfully established above it
- emit candidate returns and residues

It may not:

- originate itself
- rewrite its own governing manifold
- be mistaken for the governing `CME` set held by `SanctuaryID.GoA`
- participate directly in the governance decisions that determine what becomes
  canonical or continuity-bearing

## `ID` Issuance And Ontological Transition

`GoA` names an office in template or family form.
`GoA` with `ID` names an issued, certified, continuity-bearing manifold.
`cGoA` names a derived hot cryptic braid lane under that issued manifold.

This is not decorative naming.
It is a law of ontological transition.

### Template or family office

Examples:

- `Sanctuary.GoA`
- `CradleTek.GoA`
- `Sanctuary.RTME`

These name office possibility, template law, or family-level structure.
They do not by themselves prove issued existence.

### Issued or instantiated line

Examples:

- `SanctuaryID.GoA`
- `CradleTekID.GoA`
- `SanctuaryID.RTME`

These name actual issued lines that have crossed into accountable being.

They imply:

- issuance
- certification
- cryptographic lineage
- continuity-bearing accountability

### Derived cryptic subordinate lane

Examples:

- `cGoA`
- `cOE`
- `cSelfGEL`

These are not templates and not public issuance surfaces.
They are hot derived work lanes under issued authority.

### `ID` rules

1. No runtime surface may be named with `*ID` unless issuance,
   certification, and cryptographic lineage have been lawfully established.
2. Template surfaces never use `*ID`.
3. No `c*` surface may exist without an issued parent line above it.
4. `ID` should remain reserved for surfaces that truly carry certified,
   lineage-bearing accountability.

## Downward Connection Law

Each step in the lawful chain has a distinct office.

- `Sanctuary -> SanctuaryID.GoA`
  governance origination and issuance
- `SanctuaryID.GoA -> CradleTekID.GoA`
  lawful downward authorization into cradle-local control
- `CradleTekID.GoA -> cGoA`
  cradle-local instantiation of a fresh one-`CME` braid lane
- `cGoA -> CME`
  hot cryptic formation of one operator-bonded bounded worker office

No step should be skipped, merged, or silently inferred.

- `SanctuaryID.GoA` is not merely a larger cradle controller.
- `SanctuaryID.GoA` is the governing `CME` set/manifold, not the
  operator-bonded spawn lane.
- `CradleTekID.GoA` is not a governance-origin surface.
- `CradleTekID.GoA -> cGoA -> CME` is the operator-bonded `CME` path, not the
  governing `CME` set.
- `Sanctuary` does not directly spawn `CME`s.
- `CradleTek` does not originate governance agents.
- `cGoA` is one-`CME` braid space, not a shared substrate.
- template `GoA` is not the same ontological state as issued `GoA`.
