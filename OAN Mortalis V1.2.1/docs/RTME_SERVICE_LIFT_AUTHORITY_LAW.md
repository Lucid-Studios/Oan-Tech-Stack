# RTME SERVICE LIFT AUTHORITY LAW

## Purpose

This note defines what kind of act RTME service lift actually is in
`V1.2.1`.

It exists so future hosted motion is described as a lawful change in hosting
posture rather than a vague consequence of construction.

## Governing Compression

> supervisory witness is not service authorization.

> service lift changes hosting posture, not governing identity.

> service lift is not governing `CME` placement.

## Lift Act

RTME service lift is a later explicit act that may change hosted service
posture only after lawful preconditions are already satisfied.

It may not:

- change governing identity
- self-issue from cryptic work
- collapse into runtime autonomy
- stand in for Atlas mutation

The bounded passive authority record for this later act now lives in:

- `RtmeServiceLiftAuthorityRecord`

## Status Discipline

The intended service register remains unchanged in this slice:

- `templated-disabled` remains the current posture for the hosted
  `SanctuaryID.RTME` service entry
- `installed-disabled` remains the current posture for governed hosted cryptic
  bundle residency beneath `SLI.Lisp`
- `authorized` remains reserved service-register grammar unless lawfully
  issued by the later authority surface

No service entry becomes `authorized` in this slice.

## Authority Burden

Any later lawful lift must preserve:

- the identity of the authority surface invoked
- the precondition snapshot that made lift discussable
- the distinction between hosted motion and governing identity
- the non-grant boundary against `CME` placement
- the witness chain proving the act changed posture explicitly
