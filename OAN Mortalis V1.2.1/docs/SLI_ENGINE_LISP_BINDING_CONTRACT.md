# SLI_ENGINE_LISP_BINDING_CONTRACT

## Purpose

This note defines the first doctrine-only binding contract for the next
admissible seam in `V1.2.1`:

`SLI.Engine -> SLI.Lisp -> SanctuaryID.RTME`

Its office is narrow.
It fixes lawful approach and binding shape only.
It does not grant live runtime motion.

## Governing Compression

The seam is now named as a real constitutional edge.
It is still withheld from realization.

The working sentence is:

> binding is not realization.

That means:

- the seam may be described
- the seam may be constrained
- the seam may be refused
- the seam may not yet act as live `RTME` binding

The later RTME hosted service-lift corridor does not widen this contract into
live motion.
Carrier law remains prior to lift authority.

## Seam Office

This seam exists to constrain how engine-side symbolic admissibility may
approach hosted symbolic bundle residency beneath `SanctuaryID.RTME`.

It does not exist to:

- authorize operator realization
- authorize issued `RTME` wake
- authorize live `EC` motion
- authorize continuity widening by successful binding shape alone

## Minimal Admissible Carrier

The minimal admissible carrier at this seam is the existing
`SLI.Engine.PredicateLandingRequest`.

It may approach the seam only when it is derived from:

- a lawful `San.Common.SymbolicEnvelope`
- a lawful membrane decision standing
- lawful line-local handles

The minimum line-local handles that must already exist are:

- `SanctuaryGelHandle`
- `IssuedRtmeHandle`
- `RouteHandle`
- `RouteKind`

No raw symbolic product may approach the seam without first becoming this
bounded carrier shape.

## Preservation Burden Across The Seam

The seam must preserve the following without silent rewrite or loss:

- symbolic passport fields from `SymbolicEnvelope`
- membrane decision standing
- `SanctuaryGelHandle`
- `IssuedRtmeHandle`
- `RouteHandle`
- `RouteKind`
- engine-side governance trace and outcome code from
  `SLI.Engine.CrypticFloorEvaluation`
- hosted bundle handle and profile from
  `SLI.Lisp.HostedCrypticLispBundleReceipt`
- canonical floor-set readiness from
  `SLI.Lisp.HostedCrypticLispBundleReceipt`

The seam may tighten or refuse admissibility.
It may not erase the lineage or standing already carried into it.

## What May Not Cross

The following may not cross this seam as admissible binding material:

- raw symbolic products
- inferred fields
- module contents as engine-side carrier material
- synthetic handles invented at seam time
- `Defer` as a seam-entry decision
- `Refuse` as a seam-entry decision
- `Collapse` as a seam-entry decision

The seam also may not treat resident Lisp module bodies as if they were the
carrier. Carrier law lives in the bounded request and the bounded receipt
surfaces, not in module text itself.

## Transform Rule

`Transform` may approach this seam only after the transformed object is
explicitly re-passported before seam approach.

A transformed product may not arrive as an unnamed or inferred derivative.
It must re-enter the membrane as a lawful symbolic passported object before it
may become a new `PredicateLandingRequest`.

## Refusal Seat

Refusal lives at this seam as a first-class possibility.

It may occur:

- before `PredicateLandingRequest` is lawfully formed
- at engine entry when the request cannot lawfully continue
- at hosted-bundle or `RTME` admission when bundle readiness or issued-handle
  burden fails

The deeper refusal stages and return burden are governed by
`SEAM_REFUSAL_AND_RETURN_LAW.md`.
