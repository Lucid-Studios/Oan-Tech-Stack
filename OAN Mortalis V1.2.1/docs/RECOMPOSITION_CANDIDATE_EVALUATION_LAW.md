# RECOMPOSITION_CANDIDATE_EVALUATION_LAW

## Purpose

This note defines the first passive evaluation layer for recomposition
candidates in `V1.2.1`.

It exists so the sibling line may inspect whether a recomposition candidate is
fit to be carried forward without pretending that evaluation is already
operator realization, engram formation, or lawful admission.

## Governing Compression

Candidate evaluation is ordered review, not silent promotion.
It inspects admissibility, burden posture, recovery class, and candidate
outcome under preserved-field law.
It may retain, cleave, defer, or reject a candidate.
It may not realize the candidate, elevate authority, or bypass membrane
re-entry.

The strongest install-first sentence is:

> Candidate evaluation judges whether a recomposition candidate may remain in
> lawful preparation.
> It does not grant standing.
> It does not perform operator realization.
> It does not replace the later `engram.form-or-cleave` lane.

## Install-First Boundary

What is admitted now is:

- line-local candidate-evaluation doctrine
- a first passive evaluation contract family in `SLI.Runtime`
- a bounded evaluation policy that produces candidate-only outcomes

What remains withheld is:

- operator realization of `E -> A -> Lambda -> M`
- full `engram.form-or-cleave` realization
- ontological admission of accepted engrams or cleft children
- live runtime use of evaluation decisions as automatic action

## Ordered Question Family

The first passive evaluation layer should inspect a recomposition candidate in
this order:

1. admissibility:
   may the candidate remain under lawful preparation at all
2. burden posture:
   what remains preserved, bounded, or failed across the first burden axes
3. recovery:
   what recovery class truthfully remains
4. outcome:
   retain candidate, cleave candidate, defer candidate, or reject candidate

This is still candidate law, not ontological closure.

## First Passive Runtime Family

in:

- `src/SLI/SLI.Runtime/RecompositionCandidateEvaluationContracts.cs`
- `src/SLI/SLI.Runtime/RecompositionCandidateEvaluationPolicy.cs`

This family gives the line:

- evaluation requests and decisions

## Hard Rules

1. Candidate evaluation must not imply realization.
2. No evaluation may elevate authority, admissibility, or persistence.
3. Cleave posture is candidate-only here; it is not child issuance.
4. Reject posture preserves refusal evidence, not latent standing.
5. Defer posture preserves candidate residue, not hidden progress.
6. Any later action still requires lawful membrane re-entry and later
   operator realization.
