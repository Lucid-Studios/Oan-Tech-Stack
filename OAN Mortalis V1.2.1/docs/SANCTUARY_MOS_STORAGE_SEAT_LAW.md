# SANCTUARY_MOS_STORAGE_SEAT_LAW

## Purpose

This note defines `Sanctuary.MoS` as the lawful governance-bearing storage
seat in `V1.2.1` and formally introduces `Sanctuary.cMoS` as its derivational
private/cryptic companion surface.

It exists so first substrate, cryptic build chamber, and legal memory seat do
not collapse into one another.

## Governing Compression

> `Sanctuary.GEL` is first substrate, not final governance store.
>
> `Sanctuary.cGEL` is build chamber, not legal seat.
>
> `Sanctuary.MoS` is storage-seat standing, not governing `CME` placement.
>
> `Sanctuary.cMoS` is derivational and private/cryptic, not sovereign and not
> a second governance origin.
>
> having a lawful seat does not imply the right to occupy it as a governing
> entity.

## Storage-Seat Office

`Sanctuary.MoS` is:

- the lawful governance-bearing memory seat
- the legal/governmental storage seat
- the first local chamber in which `OE` and `SelfGEL` may later stand
  structurally

The bounded passive seat record for this office now lives in:

- `MosStorageSeatRecord`

## Derivational Companion

`Sanctuary.cMoS` is:

- derivational
- private/cryptic
- paired to one lawful parent `Sanctuary.MoS` seat
- the companion surface from which `cOE` and `cSelfGEL` may later surface
  derivationally

The bounded passive companion record for this office now lives in:

- `CMosSurfaceRecord`

## Seat Boundary

Neither `MoS` nor `cMoS` implies:

- pre-Cradle Sanctuary authorization
- governing `CME` placement
- CradleTek authorization
- active runtime governance

This corridor only fixes lawful seat standing and lawful derivational
companionship below those later acts.
